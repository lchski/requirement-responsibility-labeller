Application"><p>2.1 This policy applies to the core public administration as defined in section 11 of the <em><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/F-11/index.html">Financial Administration Act</a></em> unless excluded by specific acts, regulations or Orders in Council.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="3. Context"><p>3.1 Consistent and accurate application of terms and conditions of employment is an essential component of effective human resources management because it serves to attract, retain, motivate and renew the workforce of the core public administration. Deputy heads are accountable to their respective ministers and to the Treasury Board Secretariat for effective human resources management with respect to the administration of terms and conditions of employment within their organizations. </p><p>3.2 Terms and conditions of employment for persons appointed to the core public administration are as set out in the relevant collective agreements and other legislation and as supplemented by other related policy instruments. </p><p>3.3 This policy is issued pursuant to sections 7 and 11.1 of the <em>Financial Administration Act</em>.</p><p>3.4 Treasury Board has delegated to the president of the Treasury Board the authority to amend the following: </p><ul><li><em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=15772&amp;section=text#cha2"><em>Directive on Terms and Conditions of  Employment</em></a></em></li><li><em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=15774&amp;section=text#cha4"><em>Directive on Leave and Special Working  Arrangements</em></a></em></li><li><em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=15773&amp;section=text#cha1">Directive on Terms and Conditions of Employment  for Certain Excluded/Unrepresented Employees</a></em></li><li><em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=21104&amp;section=text#cha5"><em>Directive on Union Dues</em></a></em></li></ul><p>3.5 Treasury Board has delegated to the president of the Treasury Board the authority to issue and amend the:</p><ul><li><em>Directive on Performance  Pay Administration for Certain Senior Excluded and Unrepresented Levels </em><em>(under development)</em></li></ul><p>3.6 This policy is to be read in conjunction with the following:</p><ul><li><em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12405">Policy on Learning, Training and Development</a></em>;</li><li><a href="/hr-rh/lr_ca_rp-rt_cc_tr/index-eng.asp">Collective  Agreements</a>; Definitions"><p>4.1 Definitions of terms used in this policy are included in the Appendix.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="5. Policy statement"><section anchor="5.1" title="5.1 Objective"><p>The objective of this policy is to ensure the sound, consistent and effective administration of the terms and conditions of employment across the core public administration.</p></section><section anchor="5.2" title="5.2 Expected results"><p>The expected results of this policy are as follows:</p><ul><li>terms and conditions of employment are administered in an equitable, accurate, consistent, transparent and timely   manner; and </li><li>persons receive monetary and non-monetary benefits consistent with their relevant terms and conditions of employment.</li></ul></section></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="6. Policy requirements"><section anchor="6.1" title="6.1 Deputy heads are responsible for the application and administration of the terms and conditions of employment by:"><ul><li>establishing organizational structures and allocating the necessary resources to assist deputy heads in ensuring efficient   and timely delivery of the services associated with the administration and application of the terms and conditions of   employment, including the departmental delegation of authorities instrument, as required;</li><li>ensuring that departmental human resources systems are compatible and integrated with Public Works and Government   Services systems and with other core public administration systems.</li></ul></section><section anchor="6.2" title="6.2 Monitoring and reporting requirements"><p>6.2.1 Deputy heads are responsible for monitoring the performance of their organization with respect to the application and administration of terms and conditions of employment as follows:</p><ul><li>assessing the service delivery structure, resource allocation, human resources competencies, performance indicators,   as well as the human resources systems, processes and procedures to respond effectively to actual and potential deficiencies;</li><li>informing Treasury Board Secretariat of any major concerns or problems regarding the administration of terms and conditions   of employment in a timely manner; and</li><li>providing, as required, Treasury Board Secretariat with information that is considered necessary for assessing compliance   with this policy, its related directives and other policy instruments; for example, information regarding specific situations   pertaining to new hires, maternity leave, promotions, termination of employment and leave with or without pay.</li></ul><p>6.2.2 Treasury Board Secretariat is responsible for assessing departmental performance with respect to the administration of the terms and conditions of employment as well as compliance with this policy and its related policy instruments as follows:</p><ul><li>reviewing, when required, findings of the departmental monitoring report and the resultant corrective action plan;</li><li>monitoring departmental leave usage through reports derived from the Canada Public Service Agency information systems,   such as the Leave Reporting System and Leave without Pay System; and</li><li>monitoring reports and data derived from the Public Works and Government Services pay system, including those regarding   employment information (e.g. salaries, allowances and hours of work) and pay processing data.</li></ul><p>6.3 At the five-year mark of implementation, Treasury Board Secretariat will review this policy to determine its effectiveness and, when supported by risk analysis, will conduct an evaluation.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="7. Consequences"><p>7.1 Treasury Board Secretariat may increase the level of oversight when information gathered through relevant sources raises concerns about non-compliance with policy instruments. If the situation warrants, Treasury Board Secretariat may request special audits or reviews and may request that deputy heads take corrective action when appropriate.</p><p>7.2 Departments are required to pay any costs associated with errors or inappropriate application of the terms and conditions of employment from their existing departmental budgets.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="8" title="8. Roles and responsibilities of government organizations"><section anchor="8.1" title="8.1 Treasury Board Secretariat"><p>8.1.1 Treasury Board Secretariat supports the Treasury Board in its role as the employer responsible for the following:</p><ul><li>determining and regulating pay, hours of work, leave provisions and other terms and conditions of employment; and</li><li>entering into collective agreements with a bargaining agent representing a bargaining unit.</li></ul><p>8.1.2 Treasury Board Secretariat supports deputy heads and persons appointed within their organizations responsible for the administration of the terms and conditions of employment as follows:</p><ul><li>providing them with the necessary direction, guidelines and training related to this policy and its related policy   instruments; and</li><li>performing a community leadership role that promotes the sharing of information and the fostering of best practices.</li></ul></section><section anchor="8.2" title="8.2 Public Works and Government Services"><p>8.2.1 Under section 12 of the <em><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-38.2/index.html">Department of Public Works and Government Services Act</a></em>, Public Works and Government Services provides administrative and other services needed for the disbursement of pay. These services include but are not limited to the following:</p><ul><li>the issuance and reconciliation of Receiver General for Canada cheques and direct deposit payments;</li><li>the development, operations, and maintenance of a pay system as detailed in the Pay Disbursement Administrative Services   Order;</li><li>the development and delivery of training on the application of the technical functions of compensation; and</li><li>the production of official employee compensation data.</li></ul></section></chapter><chapter anchor="9" title="9.
