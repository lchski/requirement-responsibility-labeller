Application"><p><strong>2.1</strong>	This standard applies to "departments" as defined in section 2 of the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/F-11/"><em>Financial Administration Act</em></a>, with the exception of paragraphs (b) and (c), and unless excluded by specific acts, regulations or Orders in Council.</p><p><strong>2.2</strong> Section 6 applies to all <a href="#webpage" title="Web pages: definition">Web pages</a>:</p><ul><li>That are public-facing (i.e., available to individuals and businesses outside of the Government of Canada); </li><li>For which the department is accountable; and</li><li>That are provided through  <a href="#goc" title="Government of Canada Web sites: definition">Government of Canada Web sites</a> and Web applications.</li></ul><p><strong>2.3</strong> Section 6 does not apply to:</p><ul><li><a href="#webcontent" title="Web content: definition">Web content</a> that the department is not accountable for;</li><li><a href="#webpage" title="Web pages: definition">Web pages</a> that are 
	<a href="#archivedann" title="archived on the Web: definition">archived on the Web</a>; and</li><li><a href="#videocontent" title="Video content provided exclusively for reuse by media for broadcast purposes: definition">Video content provided exclusively for reuse by media for broadcast purposes.</a></li></ul><p><strong>2.4</strong> Section 6.1.1 is implemented in phases and applies as follows:</p><ul><li>Phase I (August 1, 2011 – February 29, 2012)<ul><li>All home pages and pages referenced from website and Web application home pages.</li><li>Significant number of Web pages that provide the most important information and services for individuals 

and businesses 
including rights and benefits.</li><li>Significant number of Web pages that are the most frequently used.</li><li>All new Web pages published post October 1, 2011 must immediately conform</li></ul></li><li>Phase II (March 1, 2012 – July 31, 2012)<ul><li>Additional Web pages that provide the most important information and services for individuals and 

businesses including 
rights and benefits. </li><li>Additional Web pages that are the most frequently used.</li></ul></li><li>Phase III (August 1, 2012 - July 31, 2013)<ul><li>Remaining Web pages. </li></ul></li></ul><p><strong>2.5</strong> Specific requirements of section 6.1.1 do not apply to:</p><ul><li>Web content where exclusion for a period of time can be demonstrably justified; and</li><li>Web content that is excluded by the <a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20/">Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (<abbr title="Web Content Accessibility Guidelines">WCAG</abbr>) 2.0</a>, such as essential images of text.</li></ul><p>A list of exclusions is attached in <a href="#appB">Appendix B</a>.</p><p><strong>2.6</strong> Sections <strong>7.2</strong>, <strong>7.3</strong> and <strong>8.2</strong> relating to the role 
of the Treasury Board Secretariat in monitoring compliance and directing consequences for non-compliance do not apply with 
respect to the Office of the Auditor General, the Office of the Privacy Commissioner, the Office of the Information 

Commissioner, 
the Office of the Chief Electoral Officer, the Office of the Commissioner of Lobbying, the Office of the Commissioner of 
Official Languages and the Office of the Public Sector Integrity Commissioner. The deputy heads of these organizations are 
solely responsible for monitoring and ensuring compliance with the standard within their organizations, as well as for 

responding 
to cases of non-compliance in accordance with any Treasury Board instruments providing principles and guidance on the 

management 
of compliance.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="3. Context"><p><strong>3.1</strong> The Web channel is an important part of the Government of Canada's commitment to multi-channel access and service delivery. The Government of Canada is committed to ensuring that a high level of Web accessibility is applied uniformly across its websites and Web applications.</p><p>Web technologies and standards are constantly evolving and web accessibility plays a major role in making the Government of Canada Web channel more effective and inclusive.  To meet the Government of Canada commitment to Web accessibility, the Government of Canada has adopted the <abbr title="Web Content Accessibility Guidelines">WCAG</abbr> 2.0. The <abbr title="Web Content Accessibility Guidelines">WCAG</abbr> 2.0 states:  "Following these guidelines will make content accessible to a wider range of people with disabilities, including blindness and low vision, deafness and hearing loss, learning disabilities, cognitive limitations, limited movement, speech disabilities, photosensitivity and combinations of these. Following these guidelines will also often make your Web content more usable to users in general."</p><p><strong>3.2</strong> This standard supports the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12755">Policy 
on Management of Information Technology</a></em> and the management of <abbr title="Government of Canada">GC</abbr> Web 

presence by providing departmental Chief 
Information Officers (<abbr title="Chief Information Officer">CIO</abbr>) or equivalents as well as heads of communications 

or other officials supporting the management 
of <abbr title="Information Technology">IT</abbr> with additional requirements to ensure consistency in <abbr title="Information Technology">IT</abbr> management processes.</p><p><strong>3.3</strong> This standard is issued under the authority of section 7 of the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/F-11/"><em>Financial Administration Act</em></a> by the Secretary of the Treasury Board pursuant to subsection 6(4) of the <em>Financial Administration Act</em> and section 3.5 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12755"><em>Policy on Management of Information Technology</em></a>.</p><p><strong>3.4 </strong>This standard is to be read in conjunction with the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=24227"><em>Standard on Web Usability</em></a>, the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=25875"><em>Standard on Web Interoperability</em></a> and the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=27088"><em>Standard on Optimizing Websites and Applications for Mobile Devices</em></a>.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="4. <a href="#appA">Appendix A</a>.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="5. Standard statement"><section anchor="5.1" title="5.1 Objective"><p><strong>5.1.1</strong> The objective of this standard is to ensure a high level of Web accessibility is applied uniformly 
across <a href="#goc" title="Government of Canada Web sites: definition">Government of Canada websites and Web applications</a>.</p></section><section anchor="5.2" title="5.2 Expected Results"><p><strong>5.2.1 </strong><a href="#goc" title="Government of Canada Web Sites: definition">Government of Canada websites and Web applications</a> respect 
internationally accepted guidelines for Web accessibility and are measured consistently.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="6. <a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20/#conformance-reqs"><abbr title="Web Content Accessibility Guidelines">WCAG</abbr> 2.0 

conformance requirements</a>:</p><ul><li><a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG/#cc1">Conformance requirement 1 (Conformance Level)</a> defines the levels of conformance.<br />
	It can only be met if the following are true:<ul><li>Level AA conformance is met in full.</li><li><a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20-TECHS/failures.html">Common failures</a> are avoided for all 
		applicable <a href="#success" title="success criteria: definition">success criteria</a>.</li><li><a href="#techniques" title="Sufficient techniques: definition">Sufficient techniques</a> are used to meet all applicable
		<a href="#success" title="success criteria: definition">success criteria</a>. </li><li><a href="#techniques" title="Sufficient techniques: definition">Sufficient techniques</a> specific to each <a href="#technology" title="technology: definition">technology</a> 
		(that is <a href="#reliedupon" title="relied upon: definition">relied upon</a>) are used where applicable.</li></ul></li><li><a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG/#cc2">Conformance requirement 2 (Full pages)</a> defines what needs to be 

assessed 
for a Web page.</li><li><a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG/#cc3">Conformance requirement 3 (Complete processes)</a> defines what needs to 

be 
assessed for a Web page that is part of a <a href="#process" title="process: definition">process</a>.</li><li><a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG/#cc4">Conformance requirement 4 (Only Accessibility-Supported Ways of Using 

Technologies)</a> 
defines the ways of using
	<a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20/#technologydef" title="technology (Web content): definition">technologies</a> that can be
	<a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20/#reliedupondef" title="relied upon (technologies that are): definition">relied 

upon</a> 
to satisfy the <a href="#success" title="success criteria: definition">success criteria</a>. It can only be met by use of the following
	<a href="#technology" title="technologies: definition">technologies</a>:<ul><li><abbr title="Extensible Hypertext Markup Language">XHTML</abbr> 1.0 or later excluding 
		<a href="/clf2-nsi2/tb-bo/td-dt/adea-sread-eng.asp">deprecated elements and attributes</a>,</li><li><abbr title="Hypertext Markup Language">HTML</abbr> 4.01 excluding 
		<a href="/clf2-nsi2/tb-bo/td-dt/adea-sread-eng.asp">deprecated elements and attributes</a>,</li><li><abbr title="Hypertext Markup Language 5">HTML5</abbr> or later excluding 
		<a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/html5/obsolete.html">obsolete features</a>, or</li><li><a href="#technology" title="Technologies: definition">Technologies</a> with <a href="#techniques" title="sufficient techniques: definition">sufficient techniques</a> (specific to each <a href="#technology" title="technology: definition">technology</a>) to meet all applicable 
<a href="#success" title="success criteria: definition">success criteria</a>.</li></ul></li><li><a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG/#cc1">Conformance requirement 5 (Non-Interference)</a> defines requirements 

for 
ways of using <a href="http://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG20/#technologydef" title="technology (Web content): definition">technologies</a> 
which are not <a href="#reliedupon" title="relied upon: definition">relied upon</a> to satisfy the 
<a href="#success" title="success criteria: definition">success criteria</a>. </li></ul><p>A list of exclusions is attached in <a href="#appB">Appendix B</a>.</p></section><section anchor="6.2" title="6.2 The senior departmental official, designated by the deputy head, is responsible for:"><p><strong>6.2.1 </strong> Overseeing the implementation of this standard in their department and carrying out the activities referred to in 7.2.</p></section><section anchor="6.3" title="6.3 The departmental Chief Information Officer (CIO) or equivalent is responsible for:"><p><strong>6.3.1</strong> Ensuring that software and systems acquired by departments enable websites and Web applications to meet the requirements of section 6.1.1 or are modifiable to enable them to meet these requirements.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="7.
