Effective Date"><p>1.1 This directive takes effect on November 19, 2012 and replaces the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12528">Directive on the Use of Official Languages on Web Sites</a></em> and the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12527">Directive on the Use of Official Languages in Electronic Communications</a></em>.</p><p>1.2 Section 6.6.3.2 regarding Website addresses takes effect on July 31, 2013.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="2" title="2. Application"><p>2.1 This directive applies to <a href="#institutions" title="See definition">institutions</a> subject to Part IV (Communications with and Services to the Public) and Part V (Language of Work) of the <em><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc./pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12316ca/eng/acts/O-3.01/index.html">Official Languages Act</a></em> with the exception of the Senate, the House of Commons, the Library of Parliament, the office of the Senate Ethics Officer, and the office of the Conflict of Interest and Ethics Commissioner.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="3. Context"><p>3.1 This directive is issued to support the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26160"><em>Policy on Official Languages</em></a> by setting out the requirements related to services as well as communications involving the public or employees of institutions.</p><p>3.2 The <em><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/O-3.01/index.html">Official Languages Act</a></em> (OLA) and the <em><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/SOR-92-48/index.html">Official Languages (Communications with and Services to the Public) Regulations</a> (Regulations)</em> provide that members of the <a href="#public" title="See definition">public</a> have the right to receive services from or communicate with the <a href="#designated" title="See definition">designated offices</a> of an institution in the official language of their choice.</p><p>3.3 Employees play a vital role in fulfilling their institution’s official language obligations when providing services to or communicating with the public or employees.</p><p>3.4 Employees of institutions have official language rights when receiving services or communicating with institutions in the exercise of their duties and functions. The <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26168">Directive on Official Languages for People Management</a></em> provides further information regarding these rights.</p><p>3.5 The <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr> specifies that the obligation to communicate with and serve members of the public in the official language of their choice takes precedence over employees' language-of-work rights.</p><p>3.6 This directive is issued by the Treasury Board pursuant to its authority under section 46 of the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr>.</p><p>3.7 This directive must be read in conjunction with Parts IV and V of the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr>, the Regulations, and related Treasury Board policy instruments, 
  especially the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26160">Policy on Official Languages</a></em>, the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26163">Directive on the Implementation of the Official Languages (Communications with and Services to the Public) Regulations</a></em> and the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26168"><em>Directiv</em>e on Official Languages for People Management</a>.</p><p>3.8 Additional requirements are set out in the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12316">Communications Policy of the Government of Canada</a></em>, the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12314">Federal Identity Program Policy</a></em>, and the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=24227">Standard on Web Usability</a></em>.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="4. Definitions"><p>Please refer to Appendix 2.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="5. Directive Statement"><section anchor="5.1" title="5.1  Objective"><p>To establish, across all institutions, sound and consistent practices and procedures for <a href="#deputy" title="See definition">Deputy heads</a> or their delegates in the area of communications with and services to the public and employees.</p></section><section anchor="5.2" title="5.2  Expected results"><p>It is expected that the practices and procedures contained in this directive are clearly communicated and effectively implemented by the institution so that:</p><ul><li>the language rights of the public are respected;</li><li>the language rights of employees of institutions are respected when receiving services from or communicating with their institution in the exercise of their duties and functions; and</li><li>the language rights of employees of institutions are respected when receiving services from <a href="#central" title="See definition">central and common services agencies</a>.</li></ul></section></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="6. Requirements"><section anchor="6.1" title="6.1  Order of Official Languages"><p>Deputy heads or their delegates are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.1.1 <em>(Order of official languages)</em> Ensuring that, when both official languages are used, the order in which they appear is:
  <ul><li>French first when the issuing office is located in the province of Quebec; and</li><li>English first when the issuing office is located elsewhere in Canada.</li></ul></li></ul></section><section anchor="6.2" title="6.2 Communications with and Services to the Public"><p>Managers of designated offices are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.2.1 <em>(Active offer)</em> Ensuring that communications and services of their offices are <a href="#actively" title="See definition">actively offered</a> in English and French, and in particular that:
  <ul><li>the necessary <a href="#capacity" title="See definition">capacity</a> is in place to communicate with and provide services to members of the public in both official languages;</li><li>the choice between English and French is clearly offered to the member of the public when communication is initiated; and</li><li>the communication continues in the official language chosen by the member of the public.</li></ul></li></ul><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.2.2 <em>(Communications with and services to the public)</em> Ensuring that communications with and services to the public from a designated office are in both official languages, regardless of the <a href="#method" title="See definition">method of communication</a> used. If the language preference of the recipient is known, that language is used.</li><li>6.2.3 <em>(Use of media)</em> When a designated office uses the media to communicate with the public in circumstances other than those covered by <a href="#section" title="See definition">Section 11 of the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr></a>, ensuring the following:
  <ul><li>Communications to the public are efficient in both official languages;</li><li>Communications are in different media in each language when using the same type of media would not reach targeted members of the public in the official language of their choice in an efficient manner.</li></ul></li><li>6.2.4 (<em>Communications</em><em>– other languages</em>) If communications are required to be in both official languages and another language is used, ensuring that all information is at a minimum available in English and French. The official languages appear first.</li></ul><p>Employees with the respective authorities to initiate, sign or confirm fulfillment of contracts are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.2.5 <em>(Third party acting on behalf of an institution)</em> Ensuring that, for any contract with a third party acting on behalf of an institution:
	<ul><li>Any such contract includes applicable official languages clauses that clearly identify official languages requirements with which the third party must comply;</li><li>Any confirmation of the third party’s delivery performance includes compliance with official languages obligations.</li></ul></li></ul></section><section anchor="6.3" title="6.3 Communications Involving Employees of Institutions"><p>Deputy heads or their delegates are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.3.1 <em>(Bilingual regions)</em> Ensuring that, subject to the requirements set out in the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26168">Directive on Official Languages for People Management</a>,</em> communications and services to employees in bilingual regions are in both official languages. Communications in only one official language are issued when using the preferred language of an employee.</li><li>6.3.2 <em>(Unilingual regions)</em> Ensuring that, subject to the requirements set out in the <em><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26168">Directive on Official Languages for People Management</a></em>, communications and services intended exclusively for employees in unilingual regions are in the official language that predominates in the province or territory where the employees are located.</li><li>6.3.3 <em>(Regions with differing languages)</em> Ensuring that communications with employees in regions with differing languages of work are conducted using one of the options listed in Appendix 1 of this directive.</li><li>6.3.4 (<em>Communications between employees of different institutions)</em> Providing guidance and taking the measures needed to ensure that employees are able to communicate with employees of other institutions who speak the other official language. In doing so, institutions respect the rights of unilingual employees and of those employees who may work in the official language of their choice.</li><li>6.3.5 <em>(E-mails and voicemails)</em> Ensuring that employees who occupy bilingual positions have e-mail signature blocks, voicemail messages and automated messages that are complete in each of the two official languages.</li></ul><p>Managers in central and common services agencies are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.3.6 <em>(Central and Common Services Agencies)</em> Ensuring that central and common services agencies respect the language-of-work rights of employees in institutions to which they provide services or over which they have authority.</li></ul></section><section anchor="6.4" title="6.4 Signs"><p>Planners and managers of all offices and facilities are responsible for ensuring that:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.4.1 <em>(Signage)</em><ul><li>Signs identifying the institution’s offices are in both official languages.</li><li>Signage regarding the health, safety or security of the public is in both official languages. This includes words and written notices as well as standardized public announcements inside or outside the institution’s offices.</li></ul></li></ul></section><section anchor="6.5" title="6.5 Events"><p>Deputy heads or their delegates are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.5.1 <em>(Events of national or international scope)</em> Ensuring that any office that communicates with and serves the public in circumstances related to public <a href="#events" title="See definition">events of national or international scope</a> provides communications and services in both official languages when the institution:
<ul><li>organizes or hosts such an event; or</li><li>participates in an event of this nature, including those organized or hosted by an entity not subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr>.</li></ul></li></ul></section><section anchor="6.6" title="6.6 Official Languages Use on Websites (including Web applications)"><p>Web managers, Web <a href="#content" title="See definition">content</a> owners and equivalents are responsible for:</p><ul class="noBullet"><li>6.6.1 <em>(Application)</em> Ensuring section 6.6 is applied to:

<ul><li>all <a href="#web" title="See definition">Web pages</a> for which the institution is accountable, whether public-facing or internal, including those that facilitate on-line discussion or collaboration;</li><li>all Web content published by an institution that was produced by or on its behalf and any other Web content for which the institution is accountable.</li></ul></li><li>6.6.2 <em>(Processes and procedures)</em> Establishing processes to ensure all institutional Web pages and Web content satisfy official languages obligations for communications with and services to the public and employees.</li><li>6.6.3 <em>(Technical requirements)</em> Ensuring that the following technical requirements are applied:

<ul class="noBullet"><li>6.6.3.1 <em>(Splash page)</em> Both official languages are treated equally when a splash page is 
    used on a <a href="#web" title="See definition">Website</a>.</li><li>6.6.3.2 <em>(Website addresses)</em> Primary domain names provide equal treatment to both official languages and accurately represent the Website's primary purpose in both official languages by using at least one of the following:
    
    
    <ul><li>unilingual names for each official language (e.g. “youth” and “jeunesse”) or</li><li>a term or terms with the same meaning and spelling in both official languages (e.g. “justice”).</li></ul></li><li>6.6.3.3 <em>(Hyperlinks)</em> Users are informed:
    
    <ul><li> that links to Websites not under the control of the institution are provided solely for the convenience of users and that the content of such Websites may not be subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr>;</li><li>of the language of the Web page being linked to whenever a hyperlink is provided to a Web page whose language is different from the current Web page.</li></ul></li><li>6.6.3.4 <em>(Language selection)</em> Users are given the possibility to select either official language for Web pages that are required to be in both official languages.</li><li>6.6.3.5 <em>(Metadata)</em> Ensuring the <a href="#metadata" title="See definition">metadata</a> for any given Web page is provided in the official language(s) used on the page, where technology permits.  Optionally, other languages may also be used.</li><li>6.6.3.6 <em>(Diacritics)</em> Ensuring the <a href="#character" title="See definition">character encoding</a> used for any given Web page supports the use of diacritics. The use of all necessary diacritics is an essential criterion when evaluating the quality of both official languages.</li></ul></li><li>6.6.4 <em>(Web pages required to be in both official languages)</em> Ensuring that, if required to be in both official languages, Web pages respect the following requirements:

<ul class="noBullet"><li>6.6.4.1 <em>(Simultaneity and equal quality)</em> Web content in both official languages is available simultaneously and is of equal quality. This also applies when additional languages are used.</li><li>6.6.4.2 <em>(Interface components)</em> User interface components respect official languages obligations.</li><li>6.6.4.3 <em>(Availability only in one official language)</em> Exceptionally, Web content provided by an entity not subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr> may be made available in only one official language if such content is:
    
    
    <ul><li>not produced on behalf of an institution subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr> and the content was not modified for its purposes;</li><li>not a regularly and widely used work instrument nor a central or personal service for employees;</li><li>not subject to a request for feedback from the public nor employees in bilingual regions.</li></ul></li><li>Furthermore, the institution ensures that by making such content available in only one official language, it is not contravening any of its official languages obligations.</li></ul></li><li>6.6.5 <em>(Web content published by an institution on a Website not subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr>)</em> Ensuring that, if the institution’s Web content is published on a Website not subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr>, the following requirements are applied:
    <ul class="noBullet"><li>6.6.5.1  <em>(Institutional Web content)</em> Web content for which the institution is accountable respects the institution’s official languages obligations.</li><li id="sec6-6-5-2">6.6.5.2  <em>(Equivalent account or profile)</em> When using social media channels or platforms, a reference or link to the equivalent account or profile in the other official language is provided when the institution uses separate accounts or profiles for each official language.</li><li>6.6.5.3  <em>(Collaborative agreements)</em> Websites governed by formal or informal <a href="#collaborative" title="See definition">collaborative agreements</a> with entities not subject to the <abbr title="Official Languages Act">OLA</abbr> include clauses setting out official languages obligations.</li></ul></li></ul></section><section anchor="6.7" title="6.7 Monitoring and reporting"><p>The monitoring and reporting requirements of this directive are set out in the respective section of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26160"><em>Policy on Official Languages</em></a>.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="7. The types of Websites include departmental sites, initiative sites, sub sites and Web applications.</p><p>Further definitions about the types of sites are provided below:</p><ul><li><strong>Departmental site</strong> (<span xml:lang="fr-CA"><em>site de ministère</em></span>): A collection of Web pages that collectively represents the department (e.g., Environment Canada's Website).</li><li><strong>Initiative site</strong> (<span xml:lang="fr-CA"><em>site d'initiative</em></span>): A group of Web pages that collectively represent an initiative of the Government of Canada. Initiative sites frequently have a different domain than the departmental domain (e.g., <em><a href="http://www.youth.gc.ca/" title="Youth Canada Website">www.youth.gc.ca</a></em> is an initiative site).</li><li><strong>Sub site</strong> (<span xml:lang="fr-CA"><em>site secondaire</em></span>): A group of Web pages within a larger departmental or initiative site, where the collection of Web pages are intended for a particular audience and whose specific purpose is to feature a prominent program or service. Sub sites may have a different sub-domain or domain than their departmental domain (e.g. <em><a href="http://www.army.forces.gc.ca/" title="Canadian Army Website">www.army.forces.gc.ca</a></em> is a sub site).</li><li><strong>Web application</strong> (<span xml:lang="fr-CA"><em>application Web</em></span>): A Web application is one or more Web pages that allow users to interact and perform specific transactions.</li></ul></dd></dl></appendix></appendices></doc>
