Identity.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="Requirements"><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p><section><header><h3>Managing and coordinating</h3></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.1">Approving communications plans, strategies and products;</clause><clause anchor="6.2">Monitoring and analyzing the public environment as it relates to policies, programs, services and initiatives;</clause><clause anchor="6.3">Developing communications plans and procedures in preparation for emergency or crisis situations;</clause><clause anchor="6.4">Facilitating open and collaborative communications among all employees in their department;</clause><clause anchor="6.5">Collaborating with PCO and other departments on priority initiatives that require input from multiple departments;</clause><clause anchor="6.6">Conferring regularly with regional offices to ensure that communications objectives are met across the country;</clause><clause anchor="6.7">Ensuring that their department does not sell advertising or publish advertisements from a non-government source in its communications products and activities;</clause><clause anchor="6.8">Reviewing all departmental memoranda to Cabinet and Treasury Board submissions to ensure that appropriate measures and adequate resources are recommended to meet planned communications objectives; and</clause><clause anchor="6.9">Avoiding the appearance, real or perceived, of endorsing or providing an unfair competitive advantage to any individual, organization or private sector enterprise in communications products and activities.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Informing the public</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.10"><p>Ensuring that communications products and activities are:</p><clauses><clause anchor="6.10.1">Aligned with the government’s priorities, themes and objectives;</clause><clause anchor="6.10.2">Objective and non-partisan;</clause><clause anchor="6.10.3">Clear, timely, accurate, accessible and written in plain language;</clause><clause anchor="6.10.4">Available in English and French;</clause><clause anchor="6.10.5">Developed in a way that considers the information needs of official language minority communities in Canada;</clause><clause anchor="6.10.6">Responsive to the specific needs and interests of regional populations and multicultural and Indigenous communities; and</clause><clause anchor="6.10.7">Cost-effective;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="6.11">Using digital media and platforms as the primary means to connect and interact with the public while continuing to use multiple communications channels to meet the diverse information needs of the public; and</clause><clause anchor="6.12">Determining when communications products intended for audiences outside of Canada should also be made available in other appropriate languages.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Federal identity</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.13">Using only versions of the official symbols that are based on the digital master files maintained by the Federal Identity Program;</clause><clause anchor="6.14">Using the Canada wordmark as the global identifying symbol of the government;</clause><clause anchor="6.15">Using the Canada wordmark in association with the appropriate corporate signature in all fields of application;</clause><clause anchor="6.16">Applying alternative forms of identification when the use of the Canada wordmark and/or the appropriate corporate signature is not possible, as with text-only applications or where layout space is constricted, such as with small digital screens.</clause><clause anchor="6.17">Using the Government of Canada signature when two or more departments are involved; in government-wide, national and international activities; or when a department does not have an approved applied title;</clause><clause anchor="6.18">Displaying the official symbols prominently, free from other visual elements and visually conflicting backgrounds; and</clause><clause anchor="6.19">Ensuring that Canada’s official languages are represented equally in the department’s corporate identity and are treated with equal prominence, including their use of the same colours, style, size and type.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Social media and web</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.20"><p>Overseeing the department’s web and social media presence, including approving its <a href="http://www.gcpedia.gc.ca/wiki/Social_Media_Account_Management_Resources/Department_Social_Media_Strategy">social media strategy</a> and official social media accounts; and</p></clause><clause anchor="6.21">Using the Government of Canada’s social media analytics and official web analytics tool to evaluate and optimize the effectiveness of digital content.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3 id="toc6-5">Advertising</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.22">Developing the department’s annual advertising plan;</clause><clause anchor="6.23">Applying the principles of the <a href="http://www.adstandards.com/en/standards/canCodeOfAdStandards.aspx">Canadian Code of Advertising Standards</a>;</clause><clause anchor="6.24">Complying with the oversight mechanism for non-partisan advertising;</clause><clause anchor="6.25">Ensuring that initiatives that require parliamentary approval or trade deals that require ratification are not advertised until such approval has been received; and</clause><clause anchor="6.26">Determining when advertising initiatives that have a <a href="https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/government-communications/buying-ads-directly.html">total media buy not exceeding $25,000</a> can be purchased directly from media outlets.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Managing partnering and sponsorships arrangements</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.27">Ensuring that agreements that govern partnering and sponsorship arrangements clearly outline communications roles and responsibilities of all parties and include corporate identity requirements;</clause><clause anchor="6.28">Ensuring that communications activities and products that are related to partnering and sponsorship arrangements are communicated fairly and equitably;</clause><clause anchor="6.29">Ensuring that advertising or promotional efforts that involve the media are not the primary focus of partnering and sponsorship arrangements; and</clause><clause anchor="6.30">Ensuring that their department does not advertise the commercial goods or services of partners or sponsors.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3 id="toc6-7">Media relations and spokespersons</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.31"><p>Designating media spokespersons to communicate with the media in an official capacity on behalf of the department, and ensuring these employees:</p><clauses><clause anchor="6.31.1">Receive training in media relations;</clause><clause anchor="6.31.2">Identify themselves by name and position;</clause><clause anchor="6.31.3">Speak on the record for public attribution;</clause><clause anchor="6.31.4">Confine their remarks to facts concerning policies, programs, services or initiatives;</clause><clause anchor="6.31.5">Respect privacy rights, security needs, matters before the courts, government policy, Cabinet confidences and ministerial responsibilities; and</clause><clause anchor="6.31.6">Respect the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=25049">Values and Ethics Code for the Public Sector</a></clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="6.32">Working proactively with the media to promote public awareness and understanding of government policies, programs, services and initiatives.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Subject-matter experts</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.33"><p>Ensuring that when speaking publicly, departmental subject-matter experts:</p><clauses><clause anchor="6.33.1">Identify themselves by name and position;</clause><clause anchor="6.33.2">Speak on the record for public attribution;</clause><clause anchor="6.33.3">Address their remarks to areas for which they have responsibility and expertise;</clause><clause anchor="6.33.4">Respect privacy rights, security needs, matters before the courts, government policy, Cabinet confidences and ministerial responsibilities; and</clause><clause anchor="6.33.5">Respect the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=25049">Values and Ethics Code for the Public Sector</a>.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Printed products</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.34">Printing communications products based on demand; and</clause><clause anchor="6.35">Approving all volume printing of communications products.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Public opinion research</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.36">Ensuring that all contracted and significant non-contracted public opinion research projects align with the mandate of the department;</clause><clause anchor="6.37">Developing the department’s annual public opinion research plan for all contracted and significant non-contracted projects;</clause><clause anchor="6.38"><p>Ensuring that the annual plan:</p><clauses><clause anchor="6.38.1">Describes each public opinion research project, including the objective and the intended use of the results;</clause><clause anchor="6.38.2">Includes the total contract value, if applicable, including taxes;</clause><clause anchor="6.38.3">Indicates whether the project will be undertaken by multiple departments; and</clause><clause anchor="6.38.4">Specifies whether a project will be carried out over more than one fiscal year;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="6.39">Submitting the public opinion research annual plan and any unanticipated public opinion research projects to the deputy head for approval; and</clause><clause anchor="6.40">Ensuring that resources are not used for public opinion research activities to determine electoral voting intentions, political party preferences, party standings with the electorate, or ratings of the performance of a political party or its leaders.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Consultations and public engagement</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.41">Providing communications advice, support and guidance at all stages of consultations and public engagement initiatives; and</clause><clause anchor="6.42">Ensuring that information about external consultations and public engagement initiatives are posted on the Government of Canada’s web presence.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Public events and announcements</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.43">Ensuring that their department does not participate in, or lend support to, partisan events organized for political purposes.</clause></clauses></section><section><header><h3>Federal elections</h3><p>Heads of communications are responsible for the following:</p></header><clauses><clause anchor="6.44">Suspending advertising and public opinion research activities the day that the Governor in Council issues a writ for a general federal election and resuming only when the newly elected government is sworn into office, unless approved by the deputy head; and</clause><clause anchor="6.45">Suspending advertising activities on June 30 in a year in which there is a fixed general federal election date.</clause></clauses></section></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="Consequences"><clauses><clause anchor="7.1">The consequences of non-compliance with this directive are described in Section 7 of billboards and other out-of-home media, mobile devices, the Internet, and any other digital medium.</dd><dt>advertising activities <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(activités de publicité)</span></dt><dd>Activities related to producing and placing advertising, including campaign planning, creative development, pretesting, production, media planning, placement of advertising and evaluation.</dd><dt>Advertising Management Information System (AdMIS) <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(Système d’information sur la gestion de la publicité (GESPUB))</span></dt><dd>A database managed by Public Services and Procurement Canada that contains project information and expenditures on government advertising activities.</dd><dt>ADV number <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(numéro PUB)</span></dt><dd>A media placement authorization number issued by the Advertising Coordination and Partnerships Directorate of Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) to a department as confirmation that its advertising creative and media plan comply with government legislation, policies and standards. The ADV number authorizes the Communication Procurement Directorate of PSPC to issue a work authorization to the agency of record to implement the media plan. The ADV number is also referred to as an authorization number.</dd><dt>agency of record <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(agence de coordination)</span></dt><dd>A private sector supplier, selected through a competitive process and under contract with the Government of Canada, that negotiates, consolidates, purchases and verifies advertising media time and space for the Government of Canada.</dd><dt>authorization number <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(numéro d’autorisation)</span></dt><dd>See ADV number.</dd><dt>Canada wordmark <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(mot-symbole « Canada »)</span></dt><dd>The global identifier of the Government of Canada, which is composed of a graphically modified typeface and the flag symbol.</dd><dt>communications product <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(produit de communication)</span></dt><dd>Any product produced by or on behalf of the Government of Canada that informs the public about policies, programs, services and initiatives, as well as dangers or risks to health, safety or the environment. Communications products can also aim to explain the rights, entitlements and obligations of individuals. Communications products can be developed for a variety of media, including print, electronic and recording.</dd><dt>consultations <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(activités de consultation)</span></dt><dd>A two-way process that seeks direct participation from the public or specific stakeholders on a range of issues to inform government decisions about policies, programs, services, and legislative and regulatory initiatives, whether in person or online.</dd><dt>corporate identity <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(image de marque)</span></dt><dd>The unified manner in which an organization, company or public sector entity identifies its presence and activities. A corporate identity can include elements such as nomenclature, symbols, colour, typography and standards for graphics. The Government of Canada’s corporate identity is prescribed by the Federal Identity Program.</dd><dt>corporate signature <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(signature visuelle)</span></dt><dd>The combination of the department’s approved English and French applied title and the flag symbol or the arms of Canada.</dd><dt>Federal Identity Program (FIP) <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(Programme fédéral de l’image de marque(PFIM))</span></dt><dd>The corporate identity of the Government of Canada that ensures that departments, programs, services, assets and activities are clearly identified to the public in Canada and abroad through the consistent use of departmental titles and symbols.</dd><dt>field of application <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(domaine d’application)</span></dt><dd>The range of items, intended for internal and external use, that bear the Government of Canada signature or departmental signature and the Canada wordmark, including but not limited to stationery, forms, signage, websites and other electronic communications, communications products, personnel identification and motor vehicles.</dd><dt>head of communications <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(chef des communications)</span></dt><dd>The senior executive who is responsible for the management of the department’s corporate identity and communications products and activities.</dd><dt>mobile application gallery <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(répertoire d’applications mobiles)</span></dt><dd>The inventory of the Government of Canada’s mobile applications.</dd><dt>media buy or placement <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(achat ou placement dans les médias)</span></dt><dd>The purchase of advertising time or space, e.g., for television, radio, newspapers, magazines, websites, cinemas or out-of-home media.</dd><dt>non-partisan communications <span lang="fr" xml:lang="fr">(communications non partisanes)</span></dt><dd><p>In the context of all Government of Canada communications products and
