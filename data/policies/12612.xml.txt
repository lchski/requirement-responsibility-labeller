<?xml version="1.0" encoding="ISO-8859-1"?><doc title="Workplace Fitness Program Policy" documentID="12612" versionID="1" language="en" space="preserve" xmlns:xsi="http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema-instance" xsi:noNamespaceSchemaLocation="PolicyInstrumentSchema.xsd"><chapters><chapter anchor="1" title="Policy objective"><p>To allow departments to establish workplace fitness programs.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="2" title="Policy statement"><p>Where there is significant employee interest and a reasonable cost option has
been determined to the satisfaction of the deputy head, departments may sponsor
work place fitness programs.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="Application"><p>This policy applies to voluntary fitness programs in departments and agencies
listed under Schedule I, Part I, of the <em>Public Service Staff Relations Act</em>.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="Policy requirements"><p>Departments electing to sponsor work place fitness programs must:</p><ul><li>accept responsibility for the payment of all costs of the fitness program,
including fit-up, operating and maintenance costs for the facilities associated
with the program. In addition, departments that do not pay rent for their
accommodation must pay to the custodian department providing their
accommodation, the market value of the space dedicated to the fitness program.
One time costs, and ongoing costs related to the space shall be paid directly to
the custodian department in the first year. For the following years, an amount
equivalent to the annual market value of the space utilized, including any
associated operating costs, must be transferred to the custodian department
through the MYOP process;</li><li>ensure that user fees cover, or recover, incremental operating costs (e.g.
special maintenance and security, exercise equipment, insurance, instructor
training, staff salaries, etc.) where these are significant;</li><li>ensure that work to set up facilities is authorized by custodian
departments and any lease arrangement with third parties for space is made with
custodian departments;</li><li>ensure that these programs meet reasonable safety guidelines (e.g. health history screening,
consent forms indicating that participants accept the normal risks inherent in
and incidental to fitness activities, competent instructors, the availability of
first aid); and</li><li>ensure that commercial contractors, if any, have adequate liability
insurance.</li></ul></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="Monitoring"><p>Treasury Board Secretariat will periodically review the extent to which
departments are respecting the requirements of this policy.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="References"><p>Risk Management policy<br />
