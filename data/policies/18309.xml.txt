<?xml version="1.0" encoding="iso-8859-1"?><doc title="Directive on Privacy Practices" documentID="18309" versionID="1" language="en" space="preserve" xmlns:xsi="http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema-instance" xsi:noNamespaceSchemaLocation="PolicyInstrumentSchema.xsd"><chapters><chapter anchor="1" title="1. Effective Date"><p>1.1 This directive takes effect on May 6, 2014.</p><p>1.2 It replaces the <em>Directive on Privacy Practices</em> dated January 31, 2013.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="2" title="2. Application"><p>2.1 This directive applies to government institutions as defined  in <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/page-1.html#h-3">section  3 of the <em>Privacy Act</em></a>, including parent Crown corporations and their wholly owned  subsidiaries, if any.</p><p>2.2 This directive does not apply to the Bank of Canada.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="3. Context"><p>3.1 The <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/"><em>Privacy Act</em></a> (Act) and the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/SOR-83-508/index.html"><em>Privacy  Regulations</em></a> (Regulations) provide the legal framework for the creation, collection,  retention, use, disclosure, accuracy and disposition of personal information in  the administration of programs and activities by government institutions.</p><p>3.2 Under the Act, heads of all government institutions are  required to identify, describe and publicly report their institutions' personal  information banks (<abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr>) and classes of personal information in the Treasury  Board of Canada Secretariat's (<abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr>) annual publication entitled <em>Info Source</em>.  The descriptions of <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> and classes of personal information contained in <em>Info  Source</em> describe how government institutions inform the public and their  employees about the personal information they collect and how that information is  handled, used, retained and disposed of. <em>Info  Source</em> assists individuals in exercising their rights under the Act. </p><p>3.3 Under the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12510"><em>Policy  on Privacy Protection</em></a>, heads of government institutions are to establish  practices for the protection and management of personal information under their  respective institution's control to ensure that the Act is administered in a  consistent and fair manner. This directive supports the policy by setting out  the requirements for sound privacy practices and management of personal  information.</p><p id="s34">3.4  The President of the Treasury Board (President), as designated Minister for the  purposes of  <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/page-23.html#h-34">paragraphs 71(1)(<em>a</em>)  and (<em>b</em>) of the Act</a>, holds general responsibility for registering all <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> and for  reviewing the manner in which they are maintained and managed in all government  institutions. In addition to this general oversight role, the President is  responsible for reviewing and approving new or substantially modified <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> for  the government institutions that are departments, as defined in <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/f-11/page-1.html#h-2">section 2 of the <em>Financial  Administration Act</em> (<abbr title="Financial Administration Act">FAA</abbr>)</a>, or may exercise his or her discretion  to delegate this authority, subject to terms and conditions, under subsection 71(6) of the Act.  In exercising this  discretion, the President will consider an institution's compliance with the <em>Policy on Privacy Protection</em>, with this and other directives, as well as with any  prescribed forms. Even if the President delegates his or her authority, the  President remains responsible for the ongoing review of <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> for all government  institutions that are subject to the Act.</p><p>3.5 This directive is issued pursuant to paragraph  71(1)(<em>d</em>) of the <em>Act</em>.</p><p>3.6 This directive is to be read in conjunction with the Act, the Regulations,  other applicable legislation, including the institution's enabling legislation,  the <em>Policy on  Privacy Protection</em>, the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=18308"><em>Directive on Privacy Impact Assessment</em></a>, the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=13342"><em>Directive on Social Insurance Number</em></a>, the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=18311"><em>Directive on Privacy Requests and Correction of Personal  Information</em></a> and the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26761"><em>Standard on Privacy </em>and <em>Web Analytics</em>.</a></p></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="4. Definitions"><p>4.1 The definitions to be used in the  interpretation of this directive are listed in <a href="#appA">Appendix A</a>. Additional  definitions are listed in <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12510#appA">Appendix A of the <em>Policy on Privacy  Protection</em></a>.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="5. Directive Statement"><section anchor="5.1" title="5.1 Objective"><p>5.1.1 To facilitate the implementation and public reporting of consistent and sound privacy management practices for the creation, collection, retention, use, disclosure, accuracy and disposition of personal information under the control of government institutions.</p></section><section anchor="5.2" title="5.2 Expected Results"><p>5.2.1 Personal information is only created, collected, retained,  used, disclosed and disposed of in a manner that respects the provisions of the  Act and the Regulations.</p><p>5.2.2 <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> and classes of personal information of government  institutions are described in a manner that facilitates the process for  individuals to request access to and correction of personal information.</p><p>5.2.3 The purposes for which government institutions collect  personal information and the privacy practices that support the administration  of programs and activities are described in the <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> and classes of personal  information.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="6. Requirements"><h3>6.1 Heads of government institutions or their delegates are responsible for the following:</h3><p>6.1.1 Establishing effective privacy practices in their institution, as set out below. These practices are to be followed when officers or employees are involved in activities related to the creation, collection, retention, accuracy, use, disclosure or disposition of personal information under the control of the government institution, including the personal information of officers or employees of the institution.</p><h4>Privacy breaches</h4><p>6.1.2 Establishing plans and procedures for addressing privacy  breaches in their institution, which include the following:</p><ul><li>Roles and responsibilities in the event of a privacy breach;</li><li>Internal procedures and communications, including timing, for  notifying the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (<abbr title="Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada">OPC</abbr>), <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr> and  parties affected by the privacy breaches; and</li><li>Notification requirements which must include a process for the mandatory  reporting of material privacy breaches to the <abbr title="Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada">OPC</abbr> and to <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr>.  These procedures must also align with the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=16578"><em>Policy on Government Security</em></a> and its related directives and standards.</li></ul><h4>Personal information banks and classes of personal information</h4><p>6.1.3 Ensuring that the development process for new or  substantially modified <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> is aligned with the process for the development and  approval of the core privacy impact assessment, as required by the <em>Directive  on Privacy Impact Assessment</em>.</p><p>6.1.4 Submitting a request to <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr> for the registration of each new  PIB, or the termination of an existing PIB, and ensuring that requests are  accompanied by the following information:</p><ul><li>In the case of a request to register a new PIB, all elements of  the PIB as described in <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/page-4.html#h-8">subparagraphs 11(1)(<em>a</em>)(<em>i</em>)  through (vi) of the Act</a>, accompanied by a completed and approved core privacy  impact assessment;  </li><li>In the case of a request to terminate an existing PIB, an  explanation of why the PIB should be terminated and a confirmation that the  records and personal information contained therein have been disposed of in  accordance with the institution's Retention Disposition Authority and are no  longer under the institution's control. </li></ul><p>6.1.5 Satisfying, for the institutions that are departments as defined  in section 2 of the <abbr title="Financial Administration Act">FAA</abbr>, the additional  requirements, as set out in <a href="#appB">Appendix B</a>, for approvals by  the President in relation to <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr>, unless this approval authority has been  delegated to the head of the institution by the President, subject to terms and  conditions. </p><p>6.1.6 Notifying <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr> of changes to <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> and, where these changes are  substantial, ensuring that <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr> receives a core privacy impact assessment as  required by the <em>Directive  on Privacy Impact Assessment</em>. </p><h4>Exempt banks</h4><p>6.1.7 Ensuring that proposals submitted to <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr> to establish or  revoke an exempt bank include the following:</p><ul><li>A description of the information to be included in the exempt bank  and why that information should be included in an exempt bank;</li><li>Confirmation that the files in the bank consist predominantly of  personal information as described in <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/page-8.html">sections  21 or 22 of the Act</a> ;</li><li>The specific exemption provision in the Act being relied on and,  for any injury test exemption, a statement of the expected detrimental effect;  and</li><li>A draft Order in Council, along with a draft Regulatory Impact  Analysis Statement.</li></ul><h4>Requests and disclosures to investigative bodies</h4><p>6.1.8 Adhering to the requirements concerning  requests from and disclosures to investigative bodies outlined in <a href="#appC">Appendix C</a>.</p><h4>Recording new uses and disclosures</h4><p>6.1.9 Establishing procedures for maintaining  a record of new uses and disclosures, as well as any consistent uses that are  not reflected in a PIB. Such procedures will ensure that:</p><ul><li>Descriptions of use, purpose of collection and disclosure recorded  in all <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> are kept up-to-date (this does not apply to disclosures to investigative  bodies); </li><li>Any new consistent uses are reflected in the relevant <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr>; and</li><li>The Privacy Commissioner of Canada is notified of all new  consistent uses.</li></ul><h4>Web analytics and privacy</h4><p>6.1.10 Ensuring that the use of Web analytics for measuring and improving  performance of Government of Canada websites complies with the <em>Standard on Privacy and  Web Analytics</em>.</p><h3>6.2 Executives and senior officials who manage programs or activities involving the creation, collection or handling of personal information are responsible for:</h3><h4>Privacy practices</h4><p>6.2.1 Informing the individual who is responsible for the  institution's <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr> of any new program or activity or of any substantial  modification to an existing program or activity where personal information is  collected or handled in a decision-making process that directly affects the  individual.</p><p>6.2.2 Informing the individuals who are responsible for managing  the institution's websites, as well as functional specialists and Web content  owners, of the need to comply with the requirements of the <em>Standard on Privacy and Web  Analytics</em>.</p><p>6.2.3 Ensuring that privacy practices are consistent with and  respect the provisions found in the Act, the Regulations and other applicable  legislation, including the institution's enabling legislation. </p><p>6.2.4 Informing employees of the legal and administrative consequences of  any inappropriate or unauthorized access to, or use, disclosure, modification,  retention and disposition of, personal information related to a particular  program or activity.</p><h4>Privacy breaches</h4><p>6.2.5 Implementing  the institution's plan for addressing privacy breaches. See <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=26154"><em>Guidelines  for Privacy Breaches</em></a> issued by <abbr title="Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat">TBS</abbr>. </p><h4>Collection and creation of personal information</h4><p>6.2.6 Ensuring,  before collecting personal information, that the institution has parliamentary  authority for the program or activity for which the information is being  collected.  Obtaining an individual's  consent to a collection of personal information does not replace or establish  authority for the collection of that information.</p><p>6.2.7 Identifying the  elements to be included in a PIB before there is any new collection of personal  information.</p><p>6.2.8 Limiting the  collection of personal information to what is directly related to and  demonstrably necessary for the government institution's programs or activities.  Personal information that is created by the government institution is also  considered a collection under the Act.</p><h4>Privacy notice</h4><p>6.2.9 Notifying the  individual whose personal information is collected directly of the following:</p><ul><li>The purpose and authority for the collection;</li><li>Any uses or disclosures that are consistent with the original  purpose;</li><li>Any legal or administrative consequences for refusing to provide  the personal information; </li><li>The rights of access to, correction and protection of personal information under  the Act; and </li><li>The right to file a complaint to the Privacy Commissioner of  Canada regarding the institution's handling of the individual's personal  information.    </li></ul><p>6.2.10 Adapting the privacy notice for either written or verbal communication at the time of collection. Notices are to include a reference to  the PIB described in <em>Info Source</em>.</p><h4>Consent regarding collection, use and disclosure </h4><p>6.2.11 Consent is not  required if the personal information is to be used for the authorized purpose  for which it was obtained, for a use consistent with that purpose or for a  purpose for which it may be disclosed to the institution under <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/page-3.html#h-6">subsection 8(2)  of the Act</a>.</p><p>6.2.12 Obtaining consent from an individual for the following:</p><ul><li>The indirect collection of personal information, unless seeking  consent would result in collecting inaccurate information, would defeat the  purpose of collection or would prejudice the use of the information collected;</li><li>Uses or disclosures that are not consistent with the purposes for  which the information was originally obtained or compiled; and</li><li>Any disposition of personal information before the two-year  minimum retention standard established by the Regulations unless such disposition is expressly authorized by  legislation. </li></ul><p>6.2.13 Including the following  elements, as applicable, when seeking consent:</p><ul><li>The purpose of the consent and the specific personal information  involved;</li><li>The sources who will be asked to provide the information, in the  case of indirect collections, as well as the reason for making the collection  indirectly;</li><li>Uses or disclosures that are not consistent with the original  purpose of the collection and for which consent is being sought;</li><li>Any consequences that may result from withholding consent; and</li><li>Any alternatives to providing consent.</li></ul><p>6.2.14 Ensuring that consent is obtained in writing or is otherwise  adequately documented, including such information as the date and time of  consent. A record is required to support verbal consent.</p><h4>Accuracy</h4><p>6.2.15 Ensuring, through all reasonable measures, that personal  information to be used in a decision-making process, is as accurate, up‑to‑date  and complete as possible. Those measures will involve one or more of the  following:</p><ul><li>Direct collection or validation from the individual;</li><li>Indirect collection or validation when authorized or when consent  was obtained, which may involve verifying the personal information against a  reliable source (either public or private); and</li><li>Technological means to identify errors and discrepancies.</li></ul><p>6.2.16 Implementing, in cases when direct collection or obtaining  consent is not feasible, measures to:</p><ul><li>Ensure that the personal information is obtained from a reliable  source; or</li><li>Verify or validate the accuracy of the personal information before  use.</li></ul><p>6.2.17 Documenting the source or technique used to validate the  personal information and identifying, where appropriate, the source, as well as  any data matching in the relevant PIB description.</p><p>6.2.18 Ensuring that individuals are given the opportunity,  whenever possible, to correct inaccurate personal information before any  decision is made that could have an impact on them.</p><h4>Safeguards for use and disclosure</h4><p>6.2.19 Identifying which positions or functions in the program or  activity have a valid reason to access and handle personal information and  limiting access to individuals occupying those positions.</p><p>6.2.20 Limiting access to, and use of, personal information by  administrative, technical and physical means, to protect that information.</p><p>6.2.21 Adopting appropriate measures to ensure that access to, as  well as use and disclosure of, personal information are monitored and  documented in order to address the timely identification of inappropriate or  unauthorized access to, or handling of, personal information.</p><p>6.2.22 Following the requirements set out below when personal  information is disclosed to another institution, to a public or private sector  entity, or to an individual:</p><ul><li>The privacy notice reflects, as appropriate, the disclosure; </li><li>An agreement or arrangement with appropriate safeguards has been  established between the government institution and the public sector entity,  whether that entity is international, federal, provincial or territorial, or  municipal, before the information is shared; and </li><li>Contracts that are established with private sector entities or  with individuals outline measures and provisions to address the following: 
      <ul><li>Control over the personal information;</li><li>Limitations on collection, use, subsequent disclosure and  retention of personal information for the purposes of the contract;</li><li>Prohibitions regarding the personal information; </li><li>Disposition of the personal information, where relevant;</li><li>Administrative, technical and physical safeguards; and</li><li>Obligations of other parties acting on behalf of the government institution.</li></ul></li><li>Government institutions subject to the <em>Policy on Government Security</em> are also to ensure  that government security standards are respected and that all guidance issued  by lead security agencies as set out in <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=16578&amp;section=text#appB">Appendix B of that policy</a> is followed.</li></ul><p>6.2.23 Ensuring, when personal information is transferred out of  the control of a government institution as a result of the devolution or  privatization of a program or activity, that:</p><ul><li>Authority is established for the transfer;</li><li>Adequate privacy practices are in place prior to transfer; </li><li>The rights of employees to access and correct their personal  information will be maintained after the transfer; </li><li>A records transfer agreement, which respects any existing records  disposition authority, is in place to establish the terms and conditions for  the records being transferred, including security considerations; and</li><li>Consent is obtained from the Librarian and Archivist of Canada before  the transfer of records.</li></ul><h4>Recording of new uses and disclosures</h4><p>6.2.24 Notifying the head or appropriate delegate of any use, purpose or disclosure of personal information that is not reflected in the PIB description and updating the PIB accordingly.</p><h4>Retention and disposition of personal information</h4><p>6.2.25 Applying the institution's standards for the retention of  personal information, as well as the disposition standards as established by  Library and Archives Canada, and reporting them in the relevant PIB.</p><p>6.2.26 Ensuring that personal information of an individual that  has been used for an administrative purpose is retained by the institution in  accordance with <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/P-21/page-2.html#docCont">subsections 6(1) of the Act</a> and <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/SOR-83-508/page-1.html#h-4">paragraphs 4(1)(<em>a</em>) and (<em>b</em>) of the Regulations</a>.</p><p>6.2.27 Reviewing files described within <abbr title="Personal information banks">PIBs</abbr>, including those of  exempt banks, on a regular basis and disposing of records containing personal  information in accordance with direction from Library and Archives Canada, as  stipulated in <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/L-7.7/page-4.html#h-7">sections 12 through 14 of the <em>Library and Archives of Canada Act</em></a>. </p><p>6.2.28  Institutions that are subject to the <em>Policy on Government Security</em> are to dispose of records in accordance with government security standards.</p><h3>6.3 Monitoring and reporting requirements</h3><p>6.3.1 The monitoring and reporting requirements  of the <em>Policy on  Privacy Protection</em> apply to this  directive.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="7. Consequences"><p>7.1 The consequences identified in the <em>Policy on  Privacy Protection</em> apply to this  directive.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="8" title="8. procedures  and processes that aim to protect personal information throughout the life  cycle of both the information and the program or activity (e.g., institutional  security policy, security provisions in a service contract for the destruction  of records).</dd><dt><strong>classes of personal information</strong></dt><dd>Personal information that is not intended  to be used for an administrative purpose or that cannot be retrieved by the  name of the individual or another personal identifier (e.g., unsolicited  opinions and general correspondence).</dd><dt><strong>creation of personal information</strong></dt><dd>Any personal information element or  sub-element that a government institution assigns to an identifiable individual  regardless of whether the information is derived from existing personal  information under the control of the government institution or the institution  appends new information to the individual.</dd><dt><strong>direct collection</strong></dt><dd>The collection of personal information from the individual to whom the  information relates.</dd><dt><strong>disclosure</strong></dt><dd>The release of personal information by  any method (e.g., transmission, provision of a copy, examination of a record)  to any body or person.</dd><dt><strong>handling</strong></dt><dd>Any process involving personal  information, including collection, correction, creation, modification, use, retention,  disclosure and disposition. </dd><dt><strong>indirect collection</strong></dt><dd>The collection of personal information  from a source other than the individual  to whom the information relates.</dd><dt><strong>material privacy  breach</strong></dt><dd>A privacy breach that involves  sensitive personal information and could reasonably be expected to cause injury  or harm to the individual.</dd><dt><strong>original purpose</strong></dt><dd>The purpose that was first identified  when initiating the collection of personal information and that is directly  related to an operating program or activity of the institution. A purpose that  is not consistent with the original purpose is considered to be a secondary  purpose.</dd><dt><strong>physical safeguards</strong></dt><dd>The facilities and equipment that are used  to protect personal information (e.g., locked storage rooms, locked filing  cabinets).</dd><dt><strong>predominantly</strong></dt><dd>In the context of an exempt bank, means  that more than half of the information in each file contained in the bank  qualifies for an exemption under section 21 or 22 of the Act.</dd><dt><strong>privacy breach</strong></dt><dd>The improper or unauthorized creation,  collection, use, disclosure, retention or disposition of personal information.</dd><dt><strong>privacy notice</strong></dt><dd>A verbal or written  notice informing an individual of the purpose of a collection of personal  information and of the government institution's authority for collecting,  including creating, using and disclosing the information. The notice, which  must reference the PIB described in <em>Info  Source</em>, also informs the individual of his or her right to access, and  request the correction of, the personal information and of the consequences of refusing  to provide the information requested.</dd><dt><strong>privacy practices</strong></dt><dd>All practices related  to the creation, collection, retention, accuracy, correction, use, disclosure,  retention and disposition of personal information.</dd><dt><strong>Regulatory Impact Analysis Statement (<abbr title="Regulatory Impact Analysis Statement">RIAS</abbr>)</strong></dt><dd>A tool used for regulatory reform that  assesses the impact of a proposed regulation on the quality of the environment  and on the health, safety, security, and social and economic well-being of  Canadians.</dd><dt><strong>reliable source</strong></dt><dd>A source of information or a data  holding that is deemed to be accurate and up-to-date and that can be trusted  and relied on for the purposes of collecting or validating personal  information.</dd><dt><strong>technical safeguards</strong></dt><dd>Information technology measures that  are used to
