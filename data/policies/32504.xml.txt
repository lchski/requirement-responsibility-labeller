				</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="Objectives and expected results"><clauses><clause anchor="3.1">The objectives indicated in section 3 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32495">Policy on Financial Management</a> apply to this directive.</clause><clause anchor="3.2">The expected results indicated in section 3 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32495">Policy on Financial Management</a> apply to this directive.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="Requirements"><section><h3>Payments: general</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.1">The chief financial officer (CFO) is responsible for the following:
			
						<section><h4>Advance payments</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.1">Ensuring that advance payments are made only under exceptional circumstances and when:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.1.1">The payments are considered essential to achieving program objectives;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.1.2">No other reasonable payment alternative exists; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.1.3">The payments are in accordance with a contract, agreement or legislation;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.1.2">Ensuring that advance payment clauses, including those in interdepartmental agreements, address the following elements:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.2.1">Advance payments are lawful charges against the appropriation;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2.2">Advance payments in a given fiscal year relate to and do not exceed the reasonably expected value of the work performed or goods and services received during that year;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2.3">Multi-year maintenance contracts and licensing agreements include at least one annual payment for each year of the contract; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2.4">Funds are not carried forward into a future fiscal year through a transfer to specified purpose money or revolving fund, or by prepaying for goods and services from a revolving fund;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.1.3">Ensuring that the immediate recovery of advance payment amounts that exceed the expected value of work performed or goods and services received during that fiscal year;</clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Payment on due date</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.4">Ensuring that suppliers are paid on the due date. A 30-day payment term is used and starts when both an invoice is received and the goods or services are accepted, unless:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.4.1">The terms of payment have a demonstrable benefit for the government if paid in less than 30 days;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.4.2">Different payment terms are set out by contract, regulatory agencies, leases or rentals; or</clause><clause anchor="4.1.4.3">It is more cost-effective to make a single payment for a number of invoices that are individually under $5,000 and due within the same week, on the earliest due date of the combined invoices;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.1.5">Ensuring that amounts owed to employees are paid as soon as possible or in accordance with the relevant collective agreement, terms and conditions of employment, contract or statute, or when awarded in legal proceedings against the Crown;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.6">Ensuring that interest is paid when the payment is made later than the due date, as required by contract or statute, or when awarded in legal proceedings against the Crown;</clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Repayment of receipts and revenue</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.7">Ensuring that money received by the government for an intended use is repaid when no longer required and recorded as follows:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.7.1">If the repayment occurs in the same fiscal year that the money was received, it is recorded against the account to which the money was originally credited;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.7.2">If the repayment occurs after the fiscal year in which the money was received, it is recorded as “Refunds of previous years’ revenue”; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.7.3">If the repayment is for revenue with special revenue spending authority or specified purpose money, it is always recorded against the account originally credited;</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Assignment of Crown debt</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.8">Ensuring that where an assignor to whom the Crown owes a debt has requested their payments be made to another party, all payments continue to be sent to the assignor until further instructions are received from the Receiver General for Canada;</clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Payment of taxes</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.9">Establishing risk-based controls and oversight procedures to ensure that provincial taxes or fees, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) and the Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) are properly recorded and managed;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.10">Complying with the provisions in the Comprehensive Integrated Tax Coordination Agreements and Reciprocal Taxation Agreements when transacting within provinces and territories; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.11">Establishing a Goods and Services Tax Refundable Advance Account (GST RAA) to record GST and HST paid or payable on purchases.</clause></clauses></section></clause><clause anchor="4.2">Senior departmental managers or delegated managers are responsible for the following:
			
						<section><h4>Payment of taxes</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.1">Ensuring that provincial taxes or fees, the GST, and the HST are properly recorded and managed by:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.1.1">Complying with the provisions in the Excise Tax Act and paying the GST/HST when acquiring or importing taxable property or services; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.1.2">Charging the payment of any provincial taxes or fees to a departmental appropriation.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Salary payments</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.3">The CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<section><h4>Garnishment of salaries and wages</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.3.1">In conjunction with the head of human resources, establishing and maintaining departmental processes to ensure the following:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.3.1.1">Timely and accurate garnishment of (i) an employee’s salary or (ii) fees owed directly to a personal services contractor; and</clause><clause anchor="4.3.1.2">Remittance of monies garnished to the Courts and the provincial and territorial maintenance enforcement programs.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Payment of claims against the Crown and ex gratia payments</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.4">The CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.4.1">Ensuring that management practices and internal controls are in place for accurate and timely settlement and payment of claims against the Crown and ex gratia payments, including the standard in Appendix A.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Payments to Crown corporations: drawdowns from appropriations</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.5">The CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.5.1">Ensuring the following when providing financing to a Crown corporation through the drawdown of funds from an appropriation:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.5.1.1">A process is in place that limits the drawdowns to only the short-term cash flow requirements of the Crown corporation (e.g., monthly or semi-monthly) and that drawdowns are not made in advance of need;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.1.2">A statement of cash flow is provided by the Crown corporation that demonstrates that the proposed drawdown does not exceed the expected net disbursements for the period encompassed by the drawdown;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.1.3">A drawdown at the end of the government’s fiscal year does not exceed the Crown corporation’s need for that fiscal year, for the purpose of preventing appropriated funds from lapsing; and</clause><clause anchor="4.5.1.4">Government contracts or agreements between the department and the Crown corporation that provide ongoing payments that contribute significantly to the funding of a Crown corporation include cash provisions consistent with subsections 4.5.1.1 to 4.5.1.3 of this directive.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Payments by departmental bank account: imprest bank accounts</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.6">Departmental managers with delegated authority to administer imprest bank accounts are responsible for restricting deposits to these accounts to the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.6.1">Funds that establish, replenish or augment the account; and</clause><clause anchor="4.6.2">Refunds or repayments of expenditures or advances made from the account.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Payment by acquisition cards</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.7">The CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.7.1">Ensuring that acquisition cards are used when it is economical and feasible to do so, subject to any limitations or restrictions; and</clause><clause anchor="4.7.2">Ensuring that management practices and internal controls are in place for the use of acquisition cards, including the standard in Appendix B.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Payment by travel cards</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.8">The CFO is responsible for the following<strong>:</strong><clauses><clause anchor="4.8.1">Ensuring that travel cards are used for authorized government travel expenses when it is economical and feasible to do so, subject to any limitations or restrictions;</clause><clause anchor="4.8.2">Ensuring that executives and excluded group employees use the travel card for authorized government travel expenses; and</clause><clause anchor="4.8.3">Ensuring that management practices and internal controls are in place for the use of travel cards, including the standard in Appendix C.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="Roles of other government organizations"><clauses><clause anchor="5.1">Not applicable</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="Application"><clauses><clause anchor="6.1">This directive applies to the organizations described in section 6 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32495">Policy on Financial Management</a>.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="References"><clauses><clause anchor="7.1">Legislation
