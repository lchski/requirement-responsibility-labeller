for the purpose of interchange of machine-readable data between departments and 
agencies.</p><p>The term "Gregorian Date", as frequently used in Electronic Data Processing parlance, 
is the equivalent of the term "Calendar Date" as used in this document, i.e. date 
format consisting of three numeric fields to represent year, month and day of month 
respectively.</p><p>The term "Julian Date", also frequently used in
<acronym title="Electronic Data Processing">EDP</acronym> parlance, is the equivalent 
of the term "Ordinal Date" as used in this document, i.e. date format consisting 
of two numeric fields, often conjoined, to represent year and sequential day within 
the year commencing with 001 for January 1st.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="3. Application"><p>This standard shall apply when describing either calendar or ordinal dates in 
an all-numeric form for interchange of machine-readable data between departments 
and agencies.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="4. Qualifications"><section anchor="4.1" title="4.1 Sequence"><p>An all-numeric date consists of the following data elements from left to right:</p><ul><li>(Calendar) year-month-day</li><li>(Ordinal) year-day of year</li></ul></section><section anchor="4.2" title="4.2 Characters"><p>Arabic numerals from set 0, 1, 2, ... 9 are used.</p></section><section anchor="4.3" title="4.3 Elements"><p>4.3.1 A calendar date consists of three elements:</p><ul><li>four digits to represent "year"</li><li>two digits to represent "month"</li><li>two digits to represent "day"</li></ul><p>4.3.2 An ordinal date consists of two elements:</p><ul><li>four digits to represent "year"</li><li>three digits to represent "day of the year"</li></ul></section><section anchor="4.4" title="4.4 Separators"><p>For person-to-machine (input) or machine-to-person (output) purposes, as well 
as for person-to-person purposes, the calendar date may be edited by the inclusion 
of either of the separators space or hyphen to partition the elements, e.g.:</p><ul class="lower-alpha"><li>1975 07 01 (space separators)</li><li>1975-07-01 (hyphen separators)</li></ul><p>but the internal machine representation does not include separators.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="5.
