<doc title="Guidelines on the Proactive Disclosure of Contracts" documentID="14676" versionID="1" language="en" space="preserve" xmlns:xsi="http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema-instance" xsi:noNamespaceSchemaLocation="PolicyInstrumentSchema.HTML5.xsd"><notetoreader><p>The <em>Guidelines on the Proactive Disclosure of Contracts</em> have been amended. The changes are as follows and are effective immediately:</p><ul><li>Amendments to subsection 3.3 and the creation of subsection 3.4 set out the new reporting requirements as outlined in the <em>Access to Information Act</em> with the passage of Bill C‑58.</li><li>Amendments to subsection 4.3.3 to indicate the new reporting timeframes for the quarterly and annual public disclosure reports. </li><li>A new data element, “Ministers’ Office Contracts,” has been added to Appendix A.</li></ul></notetoreader><chapters><chapter anchor="1" title="Effective date"><clauses><clause anchor="1.1"><p>This version of the guidelines incorporates updates effective <time datetime="2019-06-21">June 21, 2019</time>.</p></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="2" title="Purpose"><clauses><clause anchor="2.1"><p>To provide guidance to managers and functional specialists on the identification, collection, reporting and publication of contract information.</p></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="Context"><clauses><clause anchor="3.1"><p>As part of Canada’s second Action Plan on Open Government, the Government of Canada has committed to the disclosure of contracting data via a centralized, machine-readable database available to the public. This commitment reinforces the proactive disclosure of contracts which reflects broader government commitments to transparency and strengthened accountability within the public sector originally announced in Budget 2004.</p></clause><clause anchor="3.2"><p>Contracting data is gathered for the purposes of:</p><ul><li>Strengthening transparency of the procurement process for Canadians to hold their government to account;</li><li>Meeting trade agreement procurement reporting obligations; and</li><li>Evaluating policy instruments.</li></ul></clause><clause anchor="3.3"><p>These guidelines support the Treasury Board <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=14494">Contracting Policy</a>. Section 5.1.2 states that all departments and agencies awarding contracts and/or amendments, are required to submit an annual report to the Treasury Board Secretariat on all contracting activities. Section 5.1.3 states that as part of trade agreements, contracting authorities are required to report certain statistics. </p></clause><clause anchor="3.4"><p>These guidelines also support the public disclosure of contract information requirements for government entities and Ministers’ offices under the Access to Information Act. Sections 77 and 86 of the Act requires Ministers’ offices and government entities to publicly disclose contracts with a value of over $10,000, a contract amendment when it modifies the contract value to exceed $10,000 and amendments to contracts that increase or decrease the value of the contract by more than $10,000.</p></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="Guidelines"><chapter anchor="4.1" title="Identification of contracts to be proactively disclosed"><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.1"><p>Treasury Board <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=14494">Contracting Policy</a> requires quarterly public disclosure of the following:</p><ul><li>A contract when its value is over $10,000; </li><li>A positive or negative amendment when its value is over $10,000; and</li><li>A positive amendment when it modifies the initial value of a contract to an amended contract value that is over $10,000.</li></ul></clause><clause anchor="4.1.2"><p>Treasury Board Contracting Policy, section 5.1.2, requires annual reporting, on a calendar year basis, to Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) via email (<a href="mailto:ncr.gwprs@pwgsc.gc.ca">ncr.gwprs@pwgsc.gc.ca</a> and cc: <a href="mailto:ZZPDCDC@tbs-sct.gc.ca">ZZPDCDC@tbs-sct.gc.ca</a>) on the following:</p><ul><li>A contract when its value is $10,000 and under; and</li><li>A positive or negative amendment when its value is $10,000 and under.</li></ul></clause><clause anchor="4.1.3"><p>Treasury Board Contracting Policy requires annual public disclosure, on a calendar year basis, on the volume and cumulative dollar value of a contract or positive or negative amendment when its value is $10,000 or less. In addition, the disclosure of the volume and cumulative dollar value of all acquisition card transactions regardless of dollar value is required.</p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.4"><p>A series of amendments to a contract made in one reporting period may be disclosed as one amendment. Alternatively, an amendment may be reported separately if this is the standard practice in the department.</p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.5"><p>Non-financial amendments do not need to be disclosed, except when it is in the public interest to do so.</p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.6"><p>A contract option, when exercised, may be disclosed as an amendment. Alternatively, the full value of a contract, including options, may be disclosed at the time of contract award.</p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.7"><p>A call-up against a standing offer or supply arrangement should be disclosed as a contract. </p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.8"><p>A standing offer agreement and a supply arrangement agreement are not procurement contracts but are to be disclosed for the purpose of transparency. Such agreements must be disclosed either as part of proactive disclosure or on a public website. </p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.9"><p>For contracts with task authorizations<sup id="fn1-rf"><a class="fn-lnk" href="#fn1"><span class="wb-inv">Footnote </span>1</a></sup>, the full potential value of the contract may be disclosed upon contract award unless the full value is not expected to be used. In the latter situation, each task authorization may be disclosed individually. When a contract includes a fixed deliverable and another deliverable that requires a task authorization, the department may disclose the contract and task authorizations in any manner that is transparent.</p></clause><clause anchor="4.1.10"><p>The government commitment is to disclose contracts or positive and negative amendments valued at over $10,000 with only very limited exceptions, such as national security. A department may withhold information regarding the following:</p><ol class="lst-lwr-alph"><li>Memoranda of understanding with other levels of government and foreign governments; </li><li>Service level agreements between federal departments or with Crown corporations; </li><li>Information about contracts whose proactive disclosure would compromise criminal investigations, litigation, national security or public safety<sup id="fn2-rf"><a class="fn-lnk" href="#fn2"><span class="wb-inv">Footnote </span>2</a></sup>; or</li><li>Information described in paragraphs 20(1)(a) to (d) of the Access to Information Act, (for example, a contractor’s confidential pricing information).</li></ol></clause><clause anchor="4.1.11"><p>When a department determines that information about a contract is exempt from proactive disclosure under Section 4.1.10(c) of these guidelines, the department is encouraged to disclose portions of contract information that are unclassified.</p></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4.2" title="Collection of contracting data elements"><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.1"><p>The data elements in appendices A and B were developed to provide consistency to the public. As a best practice, departments are encouraged to follow the recommended approach identified in appendices A and B for each data element if possible. If use of the recommended approach is not possible, a department may use a standard practice in the department for disclosure. To provide consistency to the public, it is recommended that departments move away from departmental standards and move towards the recommended reporting standard over time.</p></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4.3" title="Publication and reporting of contracts"><clauses><clause anchor="4.3.1"><p>Starting in calendar year 2017, departments must report their contracting data on the Open Government Portal. When Public Services and Procurement Canada, Shared Services Canada (SSC) or another department or Crown corporation issues a contract on a department’s behalf, the client department should report the contract. PSPC and SSC will assist client organizations by providing any information it has on such contracts through the TBS Procurement Policy Division’s GCpedia site within five business days after the end of the quarter.</p></clause><clause anchor="4.3.2"><p>When PSPC or SSC awards a contract on behalf of multiple client departments, each department may disclose its portion of the contract. For a standing offer agreement or supply arrangement agreement, it is recommended that the agreement be reported by the department that authorizes the agreement. </p></clause><clause anchor="4.3.3"><p>Reporting is to occur for:</p><ol class="lst-lwr-alph"><li>Quarterly public disclosures of data elements as detailed in Appendix A for contracts and positive and negative amendments over $10,000, within 30 days after the end of the first three quarters of a fiscal year (April to June, July to September and October to December) and 60 days after the end of the fourth quarter. </li><li>Annual public disclosures of data elements as detailed in Appendix B for contracts and positive and negative amendments $10,000 and under, by May 30th of the following calendar year.</li><li>Annual reporting to PSPC of data elements as detailed in Appendix A for contracts and positive and negative amendments $10,000 and under, by May 30th of the following calendar year.  </li></ol></clause><clause anchor="4.3.4"><p>When the above dates for posting fall on a weekend or statutory holiday, departments may post information by the last business day before the weekend or holiday in question. </p></clause><clause anchor="4.3.5"><p>A contract or amendment not reported in the reporting period in which it was awarded may be reported in the subsequent reporting period, or as soon as practicable. Any contract or amendment included in a reporting period after its publication, should clearly indicate the omission within the comments data element (refer to Appendix C).</p></clause><clause anchor="4.3.6"><p>Information posted in one reporting period can be modified for that reporting period only when a material error was made in the data originally published. Any modified information is recommended to be clearly indicated as such within the comments data (refer to Appendix C).</p></clause><clause anchor="4.3.7"><p>Departments are to provide a nil report for any quarter or calendar year in which no contract or amendment is disclosed. Departments are to populate the reporting period and the nil report data elements within the reporting template. </p></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4.4" title="Monitoring of proactive disclosure"><clauses><clause anchor="4.4.1"><p>It is recommended for the department’s Head of Procurement to be responsible for:</p><ol class="lst-lwr-alph"><li>ensuring contracting information is accurate (including information publically disclosed on the Open Government portal) to a level of materiality that is cost-efficient; </li><li>ensuring contracting information remains representative of actual contracting activity; and</li><li>validating and approving the timely publication of contracting information. </li></ol></clause></clauses></chapter></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="References"><p><strong>Relevant legislation:</strong></p><ul><li><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/a-1/">Access to Information Act</a></li><li><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/F-5.5/">Federal Accountability Act</a></li><li><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/f-11/">Financial Administration Act</a></li><li><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/sor-87-402/FullText.html">Government Contracts Regulations</a></li></ul><p><strong>Treasury Board policies:</strong></p><ul><li><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=14494">Contracting Policy</a></li><li><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32499">Directive on Accounting Standards</a>: <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32529">GC 5000 Recording Financial Transactions in the Accounts of Canada</a></li></ul><p><strong>Treasury Board standards:</strong></p><ul><li><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=23601&amp;section=text">Standard on Web Accessibility</a></li><li><a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=25845">Standard on Vendor Record </a></li></ul><p><strong>Other publications:</strong></p><ul><li><a href="http://www.fin.gc.ca/budtoc/2004/budlist-eng.asp">Budget Speech 2004</a></li></ul></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="Enquiries"><clauses><clause anchor="6.1"><p>For questions on this policy instrument, please contact <a href="http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/ip-pi/contact-eng.asp">TBS Public Enquiries</a>.</p></clause></clauses></chapter></chapters><appendices><appendix anchor="A" title="Appendix A – Data elements for annual reporting to PSPC and quarterly publication on the Open Government Portal"><section><header><h3>1.
