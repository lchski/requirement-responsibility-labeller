Effective date"><p>1.1 This directive takes effect on December 1, 2017, including, in accordance with paragraph 8.2, for functional specialists in procurement, materiel management, real property and information management. The <a href="#functional_authority_centres">functional authority centres</a> for other functional communities will establish the effective date for their respective <a href="#functional_community">functional communities</a>.</p><p>1.2 Organizations will have until December 31, 2006, to implement transitional considerations (see <a href="#appB">Appendix 2</a>). These transitional considerations will be rescinded on January 1, 2007.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="2" title="2. Application"><p>2.1 This directive applies to the core public administration as defined in the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/F-11/">Financial Administration Act</a>, i.e., the departments named in Schedule I and the other portions of the federal public administration named in Schedule IV of the Act.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="3. Context"><section anchor="3.1" title="3.1 Purpose"><p>This directive helps meet the implementation requirements stipulated in subparagraph 6.1.1 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12405">Policy on Learning, Training and Development</a> (the LTD Policy) in relation to required training, which includes training determined to be mandatory by the Treasury Board, paragraph 6.4 on monitoring and reporting and paragraph 7.1, on consequences. It provides direction to key stakeholders on how the policy requirements relating to required training under the LTD Policy are to be fulfilled.</p><p>3.2 Providing timely access to training is essential to ensure that employees have the necessary knowledge, skills and competencies to effectively carry out their duties. This is fundamental to strengthening the capacity of organizations to deliver on their mandate.</p><p>3.3 Effective implementation of this directive results in the following:</p><ul><li><a href="#employee">employees</a> share a common understanding of their role as public servants;</li><li><a href="#Supervisor">supervisors</a>, <a href="#Manager">managers</a> and <a href="#Executive">executives</a> have the necessary knowledge to effectively exercise <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a>;</li><li><a href="#Functional_specialist">functional specialists</a> in domains identified by Treasury Board as <strong> </strong>the employer meet established <a href="#Professional_standards">professional standards</a>; and</li><li>all employees acquire the knowledge and skills that will enable them to adapt to evolving circumstances.</li></ul><p>3.4 This directive is issued pursuant to paragraph 11.1(1)(f) of the Financial Administration Act.</p><p>3.5 This directive is to be read in conjunction with the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12405">Policy on Learning, Training and Development</a>.</p><p>3.6 Treasury Board has authorized the Chief Human Resources Officer of the Treasury Board Secretariat to issue this operational directive.</p><p>3.7 Deputy heads are responsible for the overall implementation of this directive.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="4. Definitions"><p>4.1 Further to the definitions included in the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=12405">Policy on Learning, Training and Development</a>, this directive supports its interpretation by adding or refining definitions for several terms. These include the terms "employee", "supervisor", "manager", "functional specialist", "designated superior" and "required training coordinator". These definitions are to be used for purposes of this directive and are attached in <a href="#appA">Appendix 1</a>.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="5. Requirements"><section anchor="5.1" title="5.1 Overview of roles and responsibilities"><h4>5.1.1 Organizations</h4><p>Organizations are responsible for:</p><ul><li>designating a required training coordinator (the Coordinator);</li><li>communicating the name of the Coordinator to the Office of the Chief Human Resources Officer and the Canada School of Public Service (the School); and</li><li>updating this information as required.</li></ul><h4>5.1.2 Required Training Coordinator</h4><p>With the exception of training determined to be mandatory by the Treasury Board, the <a href="#Required_training_coordinator">required training coordinator</a> (the Coordinator) is responsible for supporting deputy heads in fulfilling their responsibilities for the implementation of required training by:</p><ul><li>identifying, within 30 days of <a href="#Appointment">appointment</a>, <a href="#employee">employees</a> who must complete required training;</li><li>providing information, based on the relevant registration process, to the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a> or other service provider, regarding employees who must complete required training;</li><li>coordinating, within 2 months of appointment, the registration of employees who must complete required training;</li><li>confirming employees will complete required training within a <a href="#Reasonable_period_of_time">reasonable period of time</a>;</li><li>communicating with stakeholders relative to their responsibilities and requirements, including employees who must complete required training, their <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superiors</a>, the Chief Financial Officer, relevant <a href="#functional_authority_centres">functional authority centres</a>, the Registrar, other service providers and other stakeholders;</li><li>confirming that <a href="#Manager">managers</a> and <a href="#Executive">executives</a> complete the necessary <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a> leading to <a href="#Validation">validation</a> of <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a> at least every five years using the assessment instrument provided by the School through <a href="#Campusdirect">Campusdirect</a>;</li><li>tracking <a href="#Successfully_complete">successful completion</a> of required training based on the employee’s certificate of completion or the Registrar’s monthly report to the Coordinator;</li><li>notifying designated superiors once required training has been successfully completed;</li><li>recording information to be included in the <a href="#Annual_Report_on_Required_Training">Annual Report on Required Training</a> to support the Office of the Chief Human Resources Officer’s requirement for reporting annually on government-wide implementation of the LTD Policy; and</li><li>other responsibilities as required by their department.</li></ul><h4>5.1.3 Designated superiors</h4><p><a href="#Designated_superior">Designated superiors</a> are responsible for:</p><ul><li>ensuring their employees are allocated the time needed to complete required training determined to be mandatory by the Treasury Board, within the period established by the Secretary;</li><li>encouraging their <a href="#employee">employees</a> to attend all other required training within a <a href="#Reasonable_period_of_time">reasonable period of time</a> or to complete <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a> leading to <a href="#Validation">validation</a> of <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a> at least every five years;</li><li>until <a href="#Successfully_complete">successful completion</a> of required training, ensuring that <a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">first-time supervisors, managers and executives</a> fulfill the duties of their position with the exception of delegated signing authorities;</li><li>asking the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) to grant appropriate delegated signing authorities to first-time supervisors, managers and executives after they successfully complete required training;</li><li>on notification by the Coordinator that managers or executives under their supervision have successfully completed their knowledge assessment, asking the CFO to update the delegated signing authorities of these managers or executives;</li><li>supporting requests by <a href="#Functional_specialist">functional specialists</a> for appropriate professional recognition or <a href="#Certification">certification</a> issued by their <a href="#functional_authority_centres">functional authority centre’s</a> recognized certification organization; and</li><li>ensuring that employees who did not successfully complete their training submit a plan to successfully complete the required training within a reasonable period of time.</li></ul><h4>5.1.4 Employees having to complete required training</h4><p>All employees are responsible for:</p><ul><li>completing training determined to be mandatory by the Treasury Board within the period specified by the Secretary of the Treasury Board.</li></ul><p>Employees newly appointed to the core public administration are responsible for:</p><ul><li>registering for required training within two months of <a href="#Appointment">appointment</a> based on their <a href="#Organization">organization’s</a> training approval process;</li><li><a href="#Successfully_complete">successfully completing</a> required training within a <a href="#Reasonable_period_of_time">reasonable period of time</a>; and</li><li>if they do not successfully complete the training, providing to their <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a> a plan to successfully complete the required training within a reasonable period of time.</li></ul><p><a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">First-time supervisors, managers, executives and functional specialists</a> are responsible for:</p><ul><li>registering for required training within two months of appointment based on their organization’s training approval process;</li><li>fulfilling the duties of their position with the exception of <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a> until successful completion of required training;</li><li>successfully completing required training within a reasonable period of time; and</li><li>if they do not successfully complete the training, providing to their designated superior a plan to successfully complete the required training within a reasonable period of time.</li></ul><h4>5.1.5 Managers required to validate their knowledge</h4><p><a href="#Manager">Managers</a> and <a href="#Executive">executives</a> are responsible for completing a <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a> of their <a href="#Legal_responsibility">legal responsibilities</a> at least every five years.</p></section></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="6. Monitoring and reporting requirement"><p>6.1 The <a href="#Organization">organization</a> is responsible for presenting its <a href="#Annual_Report_on_Required_Training">Annual Report on Required Training</a> to the Office of the Chief Human Resources Officer by June 30 of each year which will include the following information:</p><p>6.1.1 For <a href="#Employee_newly_appointed">employees newly appointed to the core public administration</a>, <a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">first-time supervisors, managers, executives and functional specialists</a>:</p><ul><li>the total number needing required training;</li><li>the total number registered within two months of <a href="#Appointment">appointment</a>;</li><li>the total number who <a href="#Successfully_complete">successfully completed</a> the required training; and</li><li>a description of actions taken by the <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a> for those who did not successfully complete the required training.</li></ul><p>6.1.2 For <a href="#Manager">managers</a> and <a href="#Executive">executives</a> required to validate their knowledge of <a href="#Legal_responsibility">legal responsibilities</a> at least every five years:</p><ul><li>the total number needing to validate their knowledge;</li><li>the total number who validated their knowledge; and</li><li>a description of actions taken by the <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a> for those who did not take or successfully completed the <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a>.</li></ul></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="7. Consequences"><p>7.1 The consequences of non-compliance with this directive are identified in section 7 of the Policy on Learning, Training and Development.</p></chapter><chapter anchor="8" title="8. Roles and responsibilities of other government organizations"><section anchor="8.1" title="8.1 Canada School of Public Service"><p>The Canada School of Public Service (the School) is the service provider for required training for employees newly appointed to the core public administration, first-time supervisors, managers, executives and <a href="#Functional_specialist">functional specialists</a> in procurement, materiel management, real property and information management and may be selected by additional <a href="#functional_authority_centres">functional authority centres</a> in the future. The School is responsible for:</p><ul><li>developing, and regularly updating, in collaboration with the relevant policy authorities, required training and assessment instruments that meet the <a href="#Standards_on_Knowledge">Standards on Knowledge for Required Training</a> for employees, supervisors, managers and executives, and the <a href="#Professional_standards">professional standards</a> established by functional authority centres for <a href="#Functional_specialist">functional specialists</a>;</li><li>establishing a registration process and the type of information required from the Coordinator;</li><li>delivering the required training and assessment instruments and assessing whether participants successfully complete them;</li><li>issuing certificates of completion;</li><li>issuing monthly reports on required training to the Coordinator; and</li><li>maintaining a register of employees who have <a href="#Successfully_complete">successfully completed</a> required training or <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a> leading to <a href="#Validation">validation</a> of <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a>.</li></ul><p>8.1.1 For <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a> for managers and executives, the School is responsible for:</p><ul><li>developing and administering the assessment instrument that meets the <a href="#Standards_on_Knowledge">Standards on Knowledge for Required Training</a>;</li><li>issuing certificates of completion; and</li><li>issuing monthly reports to the Coordinator.</li></ul></section><section anchor="8.2" title="8.2 Functional authority centres"><p><a href="#functional_authority_centres">Functional authority centres</a> are responsible for:</p><ul><li>developing <a href="#Professional_standards">professional standards</a> for required training for their <a href="#Functional_specialist">functional specialists</a>;</li><li>participating in the development and updating of their specialized curriculum including the required training component;</li><li>determining the implementation date of required training for their functional specialists;</li><li>selecting the service provider for delivering the required training and assessment instruments and assessing whether participants successfully complete them; and</li><li>coordinating the issuance of professional recognition and/or certification by the recognized <a href="#Certification">certification</a> organization.</li></ul></section><section anchor="8.3" title="8.3 Service providers for other functional specialists"><p>Service providers are responsible for:</p><ul><li>developing, and regularly updating, in collaboration with functional authority centres, required training and assessment instruments that meet the professional standards;</li><li>establishing a registration process and the type of information required from the Coordinator;</li><li>delivering the required training and assessment instruments and assessing whether participants successfully complete them;</li><li>issuing certificates of completion; and</li><li>delivering reports on a regular basis on successful completion of required training to the Coordinator and the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a>.</li></ul></section></chapter><chapter anchor="9" title="9. Requirements" of this directive and are to be implemented between May 15, 2006, and December 31, 2006.</p><p>Employees newly appointed to the core public administration and <a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">first-time supervisors, managers and executives</a> who attended and <a href="#Successfully_complete">successfully completed</a> the pilot programs offered by the Canada School of Public Service (the School) between January 1 and May 15, 2006, as evidenced by the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a>’s monthly report to the <a href="#Required_training_coordinator">required training coordinator</a> (the Coordinator), are deemed to have successfully completed the required training.</p><p><a between January 1 and May 15, 2006, as evidenced by the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a>’s monthly report to the Coordinator, are deemed to have <a href="#Validation">validated</a> their <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a>.</p><p><a href="#Existing_functional_pecialist">Existing functional specialists</a> in the fields of procurement, materiel management and real property who have completed any of the Fundamentals courses between January 1 and May 15, 2006 — required training for professional development and certification as recognized by the Treasury Board Secretariat Professional Development and Certification Program — as evidenced by the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a>’s monthly report to the Coordinator, will be recognized as having completed these course requirements.</p></section><section anchor="B.3" title="Requirements"><h4>Knowledge assessment for existing managers and executives</h4><p>For <a href="#Existing_managers_and_executives">existing managers and executives</a>, the Coordinator:</p><ul><li>identifies employees prior to June 30, 2006, and informs the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a> based on the School’s registration process;</li><li>confirms these employees complete the <a href="#Knowledge_assessment">knowledge assessment</a> leading to <a href="#Validation">validation</a> of <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a> prior to December 31, 2006, using the assessment instrument provided by the School through Campusdirect;</li><li>tracks <a href="#Successfully_complete">successful completion</a> of validation using the employee’s certificate of completion or the Registrar’s monthly report to the Coordinator;</li><li>notifies their <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a> once the validation has been successfully completed; and</li><li>records this information in the <a href="#Annual_Report_on_Required_Training">Annual Report on Required Training</a> prepared according to the Monitoring and reporting requirements in this Appendix.</li></ul><p>For existing managers and executives, the designated superior:</p><ul><li>encourages employees to validate their knowledge. (A designated superior’s efforts in this areawill be used in evaluating his or her performance); and</li><li>asks their Chief Financial Officer to update the delegated signing authorities of managers or executives under their supervision once the Coordinator has notified them that validation was successfully completed.</li></ul><h4>Required training for existing functional specialists</h4><p>For <a href="#Existing_functional_pecialist">existing functional specialists</a> in the fields of procurement, materiel management and real property, the Coordinator:</p><ul><li>identifies employees no later than June 30, 2006, and informs the Registrar based on the School’s registration process;</li><li>ensures they are registered within a reasonable period of time based on their organization’s training approval process;</li><li>confirms they will attend, within a <a href="#Reasonable_period_of_time">reasonable period of time</a>, the relevant required training offered by the School;</li><li>tracks successful completion of required training using the employee’s certificate of completion or the Registrar’s monthly report to the Coordinator;</li><li>notifies their <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a> once the required training has been successfully completed; and</li><li>records this information in the <a href="#Annual_Report_on_Required_Training">Annual Report on Required Training</a> prepared according to the monitoring and reporting requirements found in this Appendix.</li></ul><p>For <a href="#Existing_functional_pecialist">existing functional specialists</a>, the <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a>:</p><ul><li>encourages employees to attend required training within a reasonable period of time. (A designated superior’s efforts in this areawill be used in evaluating his or her performance.); and</li><li>supports requests by <a href="#Functional_specialist">functional specialists</a> for appropriate professional recognition or certification by their functional community’s recognized <a href="#Certification">certification</a> organization.</li></ul><h4>Required training for employees newly appointed to the core public administration and for first-time supervisors, managers and executives</h4><p>For employees newly appointed to the core public administration and for <a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">first-time supervisors, managers and executives</a> <a href="#Appointment">appointed</a> between January 1 and May 15, 2006, the Coordinator:</p><ul><li>identifies employees no later than June 30, 2006, and informs the <a href="#Registrar">Registrar</a> based on the School’s registration process;</li><li>ensures they are registered within a <a href="#Reasonable_period_of_time">reasonable period of time</a> based on their organization’s training approval process;</li><li>confirms they will attend, within a reasonable period of time, the relevant required training offered by the School;</li><li>tracks successful completion of required training using the employee’s certificate of completion or the Registrar’s monthly report to the Coordinator;</li><li>notifies their <a href="#Designated_superior">designated superior</a> once the required training has been successfully completed; and</li><li>records this information in the <a href="#Annual_Report_on_Required_Training">Annual Report on Required Training</a> prepared according to the monitoring and reporting requirements found in this Appendix.</li></ul><p>For <a href="#Employee_newly_appointed">employees newly appointed to the core public administration</a> and for <a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">existing first-time supervisors, managers and executives appointed</a> between January 1 and May 15, 2006, the designated superior:</p><ul><li>encourages employees to attend required training within a reasonable period of time. (A designated superior’s efforts in this area will be used in evaluating his or her performance.); and</li><li>asking their Chief Financial Officer (CFO) to grant appropriate delegated signing authorities to <a href="#First_time_supervisors_managers">first-time supervisors, managers and executives</a> after they successfully complete required training.</li></ul></section><section anchor="B.4" title="Consequences"><p>The knowledge assessment leading to validation of <a href="#Delegated_signing_authorities">delegated signing authorities</a> will be effective for a period of up to five years.
