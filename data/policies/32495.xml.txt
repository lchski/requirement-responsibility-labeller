					<clauses><clause anchor="3.1.1">Financial resources of the Government of Canada are well managed in the delivery of programs to Canadians and safeguarded through balanced controls that enable flexibility and manage risk.</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="3.2">The expected government-wide results of this policy are as follows:
			
					<clauses><clause anchor="3.2.1">Governance and oversight over financial management are effective;</clause><clause anchor="3.2.2">Internal controls over financial management are effective;</clause><clause anchor="3.2.3">Financial information supports decision making and accountability to Canadians;</clause><clause anchor="3.2.4">Standardized and efficient financial management practices are in place; and</clause><clause anchor="3.2.5">The financial management workforce is agile and sustainable.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="Requirements"><clauses><clause anchor="4.1">Deputy heads are responsible for the following:
			
					<section><h3>Governance and oversight</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.1">Designating a chief financial officer (CFO) to lead the departmental financial management function;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2">Establishing departmental governance and oversight to ensure that:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.2.1">Senior departmental managers and the CFO:
			
										<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.2.1.1">Are aware of their financial management responsibilities;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2.1.2">Have the necessary knowledge to carry them out; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2.1.3">Monitor financial management performance within their areas of responsibility;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.1.2.2">The CFO is empowered to provide strategic financial and business advice and support to senior departmental managers; 								<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.8.1">Cabinet submission documents;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.8.2">Financial reports and statements, including annual financial statements;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.8.3">Annual Statement of Management Responsibility Including Internal Control Over Financial Reporting, including a summary of the annual assessment of the system of internal control over financial reporting;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.8.4">Letters of Representation to the Auditor General and the Deputy Receiver General in support of the Public Accounts; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.8.5">Other documents as required;</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Financial management workforce</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.9">Ensuring the development of talent management and succession plans that:

					<section><h3>Governance and oversight</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.1">Supporting deputy heads in fulfilling their financial management responsibilities and accountabilities;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.2">Leading and managing the departmental financial management function, including the following:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.2.1">Providing strategic financial and business advice to support deputy heads and senior departmental managers to ensure that:
			
										<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.2.1.1">Program plans and key business decisions are based on sound financial analysis; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.2.1.2">Financial results are integrated into the management of program performance;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.2.2">Leading the departmental financial planning and budgeting process, including the following:
			
										<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.2.2.1">The timely allocation of budgets; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.2.2.2">The provision of tools to forecast and manage financial resources;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.2.3">Assessing financial pressures, both on an in-year and multi-year basis, and recommending resource management strategies, including opportunities to reallocate funds; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.2.4">Ensuring that the department’s financial plans, budgets, financial performance and financial position are regularly communicated and integrated into departmental strategic planning processes;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.3">Designating a deputy CFO who has appropriate qualifications to ensure the ongoing leadership of the department’s financial management function;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.4">Performing a challenge function on financial management matters, including assessing departmental financial plans, and forecasts to determine whether:

								<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.6.1">There is a critical financial risk to the department, including where there is a possibility that the department may exceed its appropriations;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.6.2">There are significant departmental financial transactions that involve uncertain or unusual circumstances; or</clause><clause anchor="4.2.6.3">There are difficulties complying with this policy, its supporting instruments or other direction from the Comptroller General of Canada;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.7">Advising the Comptroller General of Canada on a timely basis if the deputy head does not accept the CFO’s advice on a significant financial matter;</clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Internal controls</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.8">Establishing, monitoring and maintaining a risk-based system of internal control over financial management to provide reasonable assurance that:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.8.1">Public resources are used prudently and in an economical manner;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.8.2">Financial management processes are effective and efficient; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.8.3">Relevant legislation, regulations and financial management policy instruments are being complied with;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.9">Establishing, monitoring and maintaining a risk-based system of internal control over financial reporting, as demonstrated by the departmental Statement of Management Responsibility Including Internal Control Over Financial Reporting, to provide reasonable assurance, at a minimum, that:

								<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.16.1">Communicating the results and benefits of those investments, as requested;</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Financial management workforce</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.17">Ensuring that the deputy CFO possesses a designation bestowed by CPA Canada where the CFO does not possess a designation;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.18">Developing a sustainable talent management strategy and succession plan to ensure that the department has the capacity to deliver the financial management workforce;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.19">Providing leadership, both within the department and across government, in cultivating a high-performing financial management culture; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.20">Supporting the Comptroller General of Canada’s government-wide talent management and community development initiatives.</clause></clauses></section></clause><clause anchor="4.3">Senior departmental managers are responsible for the following:

The identification of a critical financial risk depends on the department’s operating environment and management’s professional judgment.</dd><dt>financial authorities <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(pouvoirs financiers)</span></dt><dd><p>Includes:</p><ul><li>Certification authority (<a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/f-11/page-9.html#s-34">Financial Administration Act, section 34</a>)</li><li>Payment authority (<a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/f-11/page-9.html#s-33">Financial Administration Act, section 33</a>)</li></ul></dd><dt>financial management <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(gestion financière)</span></dt><dd>A continuum of finance-related activities undertaken to ensure sound and prudent use of public funds in an effective, efficient and economical manner.</dd><dt>financial management system (FMS) <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(système de gestion financière (SGF))</span></dt><dd>Any combination of business processes (end-to-end, automated and manual), procedures, controls, data and software applications, all of which are categorized as either program systems or central systems, that produces financial information and related non-financial information.</dd><dt>financial plan <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(plan financier ministériel)</span></dt><dd>A multi-year plan providing the basis for identifying the elements of the work and priorities to be delivered to achieve a department’s mandate and programs. A financial plan supports the development of the in-year departmental budget and related allocations of resources.</dd><dt>financial reporting <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(rapport financier)</span></dt><dd>Internal and external financial reports and disclosures. Internal financial reporting can include financial information that supports decision making, risk assessment, planning, budgeting, resource allocations, accounting, performance assessments and reports. External reporting includes all financial statements, reports or disclosures, including those prepared for Parliament or designed to be made public.</dd><dt>internal control over financial management <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(contrôle interne en matière de gestion financière)</span></dt><dd>A set of measures and activities that provide reasonable assurance of the effectiveness and efficiency of the financial management activities of the department.</dd><dt>internal control over financial reporting <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(contrôle interne en matière de rapports financiers)</span></dt><dd>A subset of the system of internal control over financial management. Internal control over financial reporting is a set of measures and activities that allow senior management and users of financial statements to have reasonable assurance of the accuracy and completeness of the department’s financial statements.</dd><dt>senior departmental managers <span xml:lang="fr-CA" lang="fr-CA">(cadres supérieurs du ministère)</span></dt><dd>For the purposes of the Policy on Financial Management, senior departmental managers are typically departmental managers who report directly to a deputy head and are accountable for effective financial management within their areas of responsibility.</dd></dl></appendix></appendices></doc>
