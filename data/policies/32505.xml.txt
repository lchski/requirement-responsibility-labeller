					<ul><li>Directive on Receipt, Deposit and Recording of Money (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li><li>Directive on Losses of Money or Property (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li><li>Directive on Receivables Management (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li><li>Directive on Financial Management of Pay Administration, subsections 6.5 and 6.6 (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li><li>Directive on Accountable Advances (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li><li>Directive on Loans and Loan Guarantees (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li><li>Directive on the Use of the Consolidated Revenue Fund for Crown Corporations, subsection 6.2 (<time datetime="2009-10-01" class="nowrap">October 1, 2009</time>)</li></ul></clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="2" title="Authorities"><clauses><clause anchor="2.1">This directive is issued pursuant to the authorities indicated in section 7 and subsection 9(3) of the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/F-11/page-3.html">Financial Administration Act</a>.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="3" title="Objectives and expected results"><clauses><clause anchor="3.1">The objectives indicated in section 3 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32495">Policy on Financial Management</a> apply to this directive.</clause><clause anchor="3.2">The expected results indicated in section 3 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32495">Policy on Financial Management</a> apply to this directive.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="4" title="Requirements"><section><h3>Receipt, deposit and recording of public money</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.1">In relation to the receipt, deposit and recording of public money, the chief financial officer (CFO) is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.1">Ensuring that all public money received is safeguarded and recorded accurately;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.2">Ensuring that all public money received is promptly deposited in the Consolidated Revenue Fund to the credit of the Receiver General for Canada;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.3">Ensuring that periodic reconciliations are conducted between receipts and records of deposits;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.4">Ensuring that discrepancies noted in reconciliations are investigated, accounted for and resolved;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.5">Ensuring that the department adheres to the banking arrangements established by the Receiver General;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.6">Ensuring that segregation of duties is implemented between individuals who have responsibilities for receipts, deposits, recording of public money, credit granting, invoicing, collections and debt write-offs, subject to the following:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.6.1">If organizational structure, materiality or cost-effectiveness considerations do not allow for such segregation of duties, ensuring that alternative control measures are implemented and documented;</clause></clauses></clause></clauses><section><h4>Fiscal year considerations</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.7">Recording the following in the accounts of a fiscal year that just ended:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.7.1">Public money received by March 31 that is not credited by the Bank of Canada or any other financial institution to the Receiver General; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.7.2">Public money received after March 31 that impacts on an appropriation as specified in the Year End Timetable and Procedures issued by the Receiver General;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.1.8">Recording receipts of public money as refunds of previous year’s expenditures when the receipts:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.1.8.1">Relate to expenditures made in a prior fiscal year; and</clause><clause anchor="4.1.8.2">Do not impact on an appropriation of the fiscal year that just ended;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.1.9">Crediting refunds of Goods and Services Tax (GST), Harmonized Sales Tax (HST) or Quebec Sales Tax (QST) to the applicable refundable advance accounts of the current fiscal year;</clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Security deposits</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.10">Safeguarding and managing security deposits received from contractors authorized according to the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/SOR-87-402/">Government Contracts Regulations</a>;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.11">Paying simple interest on security deposits received from contractors deposited to the Consolidated Revenue Fund;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.12">Ensuring that interest paid on contractors’ security deposits (a statutory expenditure) is recovered from the Department of Finance Canada;</clause><clause anchor="4.1.13">Ensuring that other security deposits that are received in respect of any debt, obligation, or claim are managed according to the legislation that authorizes the acceptance of such security deposits; and</clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Fraudulent receipt</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.1.14">Notifying the departmental Chief Security Officer immediately of any suspected fraudulent receipts.</clause></clauses></section></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Losses of public money or property</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.2">In relation to the losses of public money or property, the CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.1">Establishing, monitoring and maintaining a risk-based departmental system of internal control to prevent losses of public money and property and detect them in a timely manner;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.2">Ensuring that losses of public money and property are promptly and accurately recorded in the accounts of the department for applicable transactions;</clause></clauses><section><h4>Recovery of loss</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.3">Ensuring that a claim is prepared against those responsible for a loss of public money or property when the loss is recoverable;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.4">Including the following elements in the claim, when applicable, in addition to the value of the loss of public money or property calculated as specified in the Public Accounts Instructions issued by the Receiver General:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.4.1">The financing costs associated with the loss of public money that involves a fraudulent receipt or an unauthorized expenditure;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.4.2">Additional costs incurred or revenue lost as a result of public property not being available for its intended purpose;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.4.3">The cost of recovery proceedings or enforced recovery; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.4.4">The GST/HST, the QST, PST and any other applicable taxes, levies or duties;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.5">Taking action to promptly recover any losses or shortages, including a cash shortage in petty cash, from the holder or custodian of an accountable advance unless all of the following conditions have been met:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.5.1">The prescribed procedures were followed and the public money was properly secured; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.5.2">No act or omission on the part of the holder or custodian contributed to the loss;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.6">Ensuring, when a manager of the holder or custodian of the advance assumes full or partial responsibility for the loss or shortage, that the following is confirmed in writing:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.6.1">The manager failed to ensure that adequate facilities and procedures were in place;</clause><clause anchor="4.2.6.2">The manager failed to provide adequate direction, instruction or supervision; or</clause><clause anchor="4.2.6.3">The manager allowed inappropriate practices to occur;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.2.7">Recovering any losses or shortages promptly from the holder or custodian of the advance and/or that individual’s manager, when both share responsibility, and apportioning the recovery based on their respective culpability; and</clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Charging of loss</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.2.8">Charging the loss of public money to a departmental appropriation when a disbursement is required to replenish a petty cash or change fund, if:
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.2.8.1">The loss is not the responsibility of the holder or custodian of the advance according to the conditions provided in subsection 4.2.5 of this directive; and</clause><clause anchor="4.2.8.2">Approval is provided by a person with the appropriate delegated signing authority.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Receivables</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.3">In relation to receivables, the CFO or the senior departmental managers designated by the deputy head are responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.3.1">Ensuring that receivables are minimized, to the extent possible, by requiring payment in advance or at the time that goods and services are provided;</clause><clause anchor="4.3.2">Granting credit only when it is an operational requirement;</clause><clause anchor="4.3.3">Ensuring that debtors are fairly treated and informed of their rights and obligations under applicable acts, regulations, policies and directives;</clause><clause anchor="4.3.4">Ensuring that all receivable transactions and related allowances for doubtful accounts are promptly and accurately recorded in the accounts of the department;</clause></clauses><section><h4>Collection</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.3.5">Taking timely and cost-effective collection actions to pursue receivables, such as the use of a set-off;</clause><clause anchor="4.3.6">Ensuring that upon request by another department or agent Crown corporation, the following information concerning a person that has a debt due to the Crown is provided to it (unless specifically prohibited by departmental or program legislation):
			
									<clauses><clause anchor="4.3.6.1">The person’s last known address and telephone number;</clause><clause anchor="4.3.6.2">The name and address of the person’s last known employer; and</clause><clause anchor="4.3.6.3">Any payment coming due to the person; and</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h4>Debt deletion</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.3.7">Taking timely action for write-off, remission or forgiveness of debts, or waiver of interests or administrative charges, when a receivable is not settled in full.</clause></clauses></section></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Employee departure process</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.4">In relation to the employee departure process, the CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.4.1">Ensuring, as part of a departure process, that an employee returns all public money owed, including outstanding accountable advances and any public property, before leaving the department; and</clause><clause anchor="4.4.2">Ensuring, in collaboration with compensation services, that when a departing employee owes public money or has not accounted for public property, that a process is put in place to take recovery action and, if necessary, establish an account receivable.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Accountable advances</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.5">In relation to accountable advances, the CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.5.1">Ensuring that accountable advances are issued only when required, used for the purpose for which they were issued, properly safeguarded, and reported on a timely basis;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.2">Seeking authority from Public Services and Procurement Canada to establish a standing advance unless separate authorities are available through departmental or program legislation;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.3">Charging temporary accountable advances to a departmental appropriation;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.4">Replenishing standing advances to their original value unless a decrease, increase or complete recovery is justified;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.5">Ensuring that segregation of duties is implemented between individuals who are responsible for managing accountable advances, maintaining accounts receivable records, and verifying and certifying accounts for payment and requisition payments, subject to the following:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.5.5.1">If organizational structure, materiality or cost-effectiveness considerations do not allow such segregation of duties, ensuring that alternative control measures are implemented and documented;</clause></clauses></clause></clauses><section><h4>Petty cash and change fund</h4><clauses><clause anchor="4.5.6">Ensuring that petty cash funds are not used to make change and that petty cash or change funds are not used to provide salary advances, cash cheques or make loans;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.7">Ensuring that petty cash expenditures are not split into two or more transactions in an attempt to avoid the limits prescribed by the <a href="http://laws.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/SOR-86-438/">Accountable Advances Regulations</a>;</clause><clause anchor="4.5.8">Ensuring that the custody of a petty cash or change fund is assigned to an individual and that an acknowledgement of responsibilities for the fund is documented; and</clause><clause anchor="4.5.9">Ensuring that petty cash or change fund accounts are reconciled when a transfer between fund custodians takes place.</clause></clauses></section></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Loans and loan guarantees</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.6">In relation to loans and loan guarantees, the CFO is responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.6.1">Reviewing the terms and conditions of loans and loan guarantees before seeking approval from the Minister of Finance when the authorizing legislation does not detail the terms and conditions or the authority to modify terms and conditions.</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.7">In relation to loans and loan guarantees, senior departmental managers are responsible for the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.7.1">Ensuring that terms and conditions for loans and loan guarantees are established, documented and appropriately authorized;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.2">Ensuring that the terms and conditions not detailed in authorizing legislation for:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.7.2.1">Loan programs for a class of recipients and loans for named recipients are approved by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance; and</clause><clause anchor="4.7.2.2">Loan guarantee programs for a class of recipients and loan guarantees for named recipients are approved by the Minister of Finance;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.7.3">Ensuring that loans and loan guarantees are structured to cover potential losses from non-performing loans, when appropriate and feasible;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.4">Undertaking a periodic review of loan and loan guarantee program terms and conditions to validate a program’s continuing relevance and operating criteria;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.5">Conducting, in collaboration with the CFO, a periodic review of all loans and loan guarantees to determine the following:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.7.5.1">Compliance with agreement terms and conditions;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.5.2">A forecast of repayment potential;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.5.3">Provision for bad debt and any amounts to be written off;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.5.4">Appropriate accounting and reporting; and</clause><clause anchor="4.7.5.5">Any valuation adjustments required;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.7.6">Ensuring that the net balance owing to the government from each loan recipient, including a repayment schedule and applicable interest provisions, is readily accessible in accounting records;</clause><clause anchor="4.7.7">Applying the amount of any repayment to the entire sum of interest due at the time of each instalment, and then to the principal (unless the loan terms and conditions specify otherwise);</clause><clause anchor="4.7.8">Ensuring that the appropriate instrument is used to obtain parliamentary authorization of loan guarantees, i.e.:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.7.8.1">Loan guarantees for named recipients that can be listed individually in the Estimates may be authorized through an appropriation act; and</clause><clause anchor="4.7.8.2">Loan guarantee programs are authorized through separate program legislation;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.7.9">Ensuring that loan guarantee agreements include a provision whereby actual or expected defaults are communicated by the lenders to departmental officials in a timely manner; and
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.7.9.1">When advised of an actual or expected default, follow-up action is to be taken immediately to ensure that the loan is recovered to the fullest extent possible.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section><section><h3>Use of the Consolidated Revenue Fund for Crown corporations</h3><clauses><clause anchor="4.8">In relation to the use of the Consolidated Revenue Fund for Crown corporations, a senior officer of Department of Finance Canada, designated by the deputy head, is responsible for ensuring the following:
			
						<clauses><clause anchor="4.8.1">Loans, advances and investments by the Government of Canada to a Crown corporation are authorized by legislation through an appropriation act or other act of Parliament;</clause><clause anchor="4.8.2">Loans and advances to a Crown corporation are made only when the following criteria are met:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.8.2.1">There is reasonable certainty that the principal will be repaid; and</clause><clause anchor="4.8.2.2">An acceptable rate of interest for loans, as established by the Minister of Finance, can be paid without seeking further parliamentary appropriations;</clause></clauses></clause><clause anchor="4.8.3">The deferment of a loan repayment until the start of operations is assessed where loans for capital expenditures to a Crown corporation are made before the relevant construction is completed; and</clause><clause anchor="4.8.4">Investments in a Crown corporation are made only when the following criteria are met:
			
								<clauses><clause anchor="4.8.4.1">The Crown corporation is self-sustaining;</clause><clause anchor="4.8.4.2">A return that is equivalent to the Crown’s cost of borrowing will be earned over time; and</clause><clause anchor="4.8.4.3">There is reasonable certainty that the investment will be recovered.</clause></clauses></clause></clauses></clause></clauses></section></chapter><chapter anchor="5" title="Roles of other government organizations"><clauses><clause anchor="5.1">Not applicable.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="6" title="Application"><clauses><clause anchor="6.1">This directive applies to the organizations listed in section 6 of the <a href="/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32495">Policy on Financial Management</a>.</clause></clauses></chapter><chapter anchor="7" title="References"><clauses><clause anchor="7.1">Legislation
